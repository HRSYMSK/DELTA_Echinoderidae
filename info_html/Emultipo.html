Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9 andmidlateral tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in subdorsal position on segment 2 and laterodorsal positionon segments 4– 9. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines long and thin. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

Based on the spine/tube pattern, the species resembles toseven Echinoderes species: E. apex, E. arlis, E. bermudensis, E.
hamiltonorum, E.
joyceae, E. schwieringae and E.
wallaceae.
However, E. multiporus can be easily discriminate from them, except for E. schwieringae, in the presence of type-2 gland cell outlets on dorsal side on most of the segments.
Both E. multiporusand E. schwieringae possess many type 2 gland cell outlets on dorsal side, however, the position of them aredifferent between the two species: those on segment 4 are inlaterodorsal position in E. multiporus, whereas in subdorsal positionin E. schwieringae, and those on segment 6 are present E. multiporus, whereas absent in E. schwieringae.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from 906-991 m depth on the Eratosthenes Seamount in the Mediterranean Sea (type locality), and 565 m depth on the Senghor Seamount in the NE Atlantic Ocean.
