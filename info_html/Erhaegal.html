Short description

Slender species with middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, tubes in sublateral and ventrolateral (presence/absence of ventrolateral ones varies within populations though) positions on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on

segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Tergal extensions are relatively long and slender. Lateral terminal spines acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plates small.

Key characters and similar species

can be identified by its spine/tube pattern combined with the absence of glandular cell outlets type 2. Precise identification can be obtained despite the intraspecific variation regarding the presence or absence of ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, in other words: the spine/tube pattern combined with absence of glandular outlets is unique no matter if ventrolateral tubes are present or absent.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from Svalbard, and has been collected from Spitsbergen fjords as well asopen water stations around Svalbard.
