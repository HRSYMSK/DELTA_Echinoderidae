Only five

Only fiveother species of Echinoderes have the combined presence of laterodorsal and lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, i.e., E. abbreviatus Higgins, 1983, E. belenae Pardos et al., 2016, E. brevipes Cepeda et al., 2019, E. rociae Pardos et al., 2016, and E. intermedius Sørensen, 2006. However, the four first mentioned species all have short and stout lateral terminal spines, which makes them very easy to distinguish from E. kathleenhannae. Only E. intermedius

show some resemblance with the new species, but the presence midlateral glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment 2, and lateral accessory tubes on segments 6 and 7 still makes it differ considerably from the new species.

Distribution and habitat

Peninsula at 630 to 700 m depth.
