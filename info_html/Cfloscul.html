Short description

Very minute (139-209 µ m) and laterally compressed species with well-developed tergosternal junctions and partially developed, intracuticular, midsternal line in segment 2. Middorsal spines are present on segments 4 to 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, and midlateral spines on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 not present. Pectinate fringes of sternal plates on segments 3 to 9 are verylong, covering anterior half of sternal plates on following segment.
Small flosculus-like sensory spots are present posteriorly on midventral junction of each segment from segments 3 to 8 (best viewed with SEM). Tergal extensions are very short and triangu lar. Lateral terminal spines are long and acicular; females with thin lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

The two species can only be distinguished by the small midventral floscula, appar ently present in C. flosculosa only, and by minor differences in sensory spot distribution, inclusive presence paradorsal sensory spots on segment 9 andlack of paradorsal sensory spots onsegment 10, characteristic for C. flosculosa, opposite to lackparadorsal sensory spots on segment 9 and presence ofparadorsal sensory spots onsegment 10, characteristic for C. liticola.
