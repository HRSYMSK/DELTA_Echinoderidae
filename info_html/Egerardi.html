Key characters and similar species

Echinoderes dujardinii species group that includes species with the spine and tube pattern described above (except for the presence of lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, whichare present in E. dujardiniiand E. gerardi only)

, laterodorsal/midlateral glandular cell outlets type 2 on segments 8 and/or 9, short triangular tergal extensions, and females with female papillae on some sternal plates. Within this species group only two species, E. dujardiniiand E. gerardi have lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, hence, this is the main character to distinguish these particular species from the rest.
E. gerardican only be distinguished from E. dujardiniiby the relative length of its middorsal spines. Whereas E. gerardihas extremely short middorsal spines where none of them are in any way close to reach the posterior margin of their respective segments, the middorsal spines on segments 7 and 8 in E. dujardinii might actually reach the posterior margins of their respective segments.

Distribution and habitat

wasdescribed from the Gulf of Tunis in the Mediterranean, and has subsequently been report from the Mediterranean coast of Turkey and from Italy. Previous reports of E. dujardinii from Spain and Turkey have also subsequently turned out to identical with E. gerardi, and it is not unlikely that the species exists through the Mediterranean Sea.
