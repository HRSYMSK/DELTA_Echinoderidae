Short description

Species with plump-shaped trunk, middorsal spine on segment 4, lateroventral tubes on segments 5 and 8, sublateral spines on segments 6 and 7, midlateral tubes on segment 9 andlaterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present insubdorsal, laterodorsal andventrolateral position on segment 2, subdorsal position on segment 4, and midlateral position on segments 5-8. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines short and stout.
Sieve plate large.

Key characters and similar species

The species resembles to E. annaeand E. rexin having the middorsal spine only on segment 4 and short lateral terminal spines. However, E.
serratulus differs from E. annae in the presence of sublateral acicular spines on segments 6 and 7; from E. rex inthe presence of tubes on segment 9, type-2 gland cell outlets on segment 5, stout lateral terminal spines, and the absence of type 2 gland cell outlets on segment 3.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from its type locality, muddy sediment at 10 m depth near Hon Mieu Island, Nha Trang, Vietnam.
