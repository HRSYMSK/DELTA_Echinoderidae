Short description

Meristoderes species with tergosternal and midsternal junctions in segment 2 appearing as indistinct lines on the cuticular surface (most easily visualized in SEM). Middorsal spine ispresent on segment 4 only, and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubeson segment 5, and lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present in subdorsal and sublateral positions on segment 2. Tergal extensions arelong and triangular. Lateral terminal spines acicular; females with rather short and thin lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

distinguished from most other species by its combined spine/tube pattern and distribution of glandular cell outlets type 2.
However, in terms of spines, tubes and glandular cell outlets type 2 the species is similar with E. charlotteae. In order to distinguish the two species, one has to look for the partial plate differentiation on segment 2, present in M. galatheaebut not in E. charlotteae, the more slender, parallel sided trunk shape in M. galatheae opposite to a trunk being broader in the anterior end in E. charlotteae, longer and more slender tergal extensions in E. charlotteae, and general differences in distribution of sensory spots.

Distribution and habitat

is described from subtidal coral sands from the Island Ghizo, in the Solomon Islands.
