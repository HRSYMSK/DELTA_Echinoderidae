Short description

Relatively chubby species with segment 1 being conspicuously narrow compared to following segments. Middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segments 2 and 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present on segment 2 (very distinct) in sublateral positions, segments 4 to 6 in subdorsal positions, and segments 8 and 9 in midlateral positions (not mentioned in original description). Tergal extensions are rounded and consisting of fringes; sternal extensions are nearly as long as tergal ones, well-developed and broadly rounded. Lateral terminal spines are short and conspicuously stout, and females also with stout lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

spine pattern combined with its short and stout lateral terminal spines is unique among congeners, hence, this alone should be sufficient to distinguish the species – especially if also combined with the distribution of glandular cell outlets type 2. The species resembles E. augustae and E. barbadensis, but these species have lateral accessory/sublateral tubes on segment 8 also and subdorsal glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment 2.

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality – a submarine cave (Jims Cave) near Tuncurry in New South Wales, Australia. It is uncertain whether the species is entirely troglobiontic or if it also lives in sediments outside the cave.
