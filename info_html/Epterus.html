Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Tufts of long cuticular hairs present in laterodorsal position on segment 9. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines long and thin.
Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

Although the spine/tube pattern of E. pterusis relatively common one in Echinoderes, the speciescan be easily recognized from the congeners by the presence of the tufts of long cuticular hairs in laterodorsal position on segment 9.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known fromvarious deep-sea and seamount areas in Arctic Sea, Atlantic Ocean and Mediterranean Sea: 903 m depth on the Karasik Seamount (Arctic Sea), 790-1656 m depth in north of the Svalbard Island (Arctic Sea), 2721 m depth on the Sedlo Seamount (Atlantic Ocean), 2789-4403 m depth on the Meditteranean Basin (Mediterranean Sea), and 675-2043 m depth on the Anaximenes Seamount (Mediterranean Sea).
Recently, the species was also reported from 4050 m depth on the continental slope of Chile, in the Southeast Pacific near the Atacama Trench (Grzelak et al. 2021). Despite the great distance between this locality and the species type locality, it was impossible to identify any morphological differences between the speci mens of the localities.
