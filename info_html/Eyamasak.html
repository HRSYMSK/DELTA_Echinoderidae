Short description

Slender species with conspicuously long, acicular tergal extensions, belonging to the E. spinifurca species group.
Middorsal spines are present on segments 4 to 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and midlateral tubes on segment 10. All tubes are quite long and well-developed. Very small gland ular cell outlets type 2 are present in subdorsal positions on segment 2, and in laterodorsal positions on segments 8 and 9. Lateral terminal spines are acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines and female papillae on sternal plates of segments 7 and 8. Tergal extensions are long, slender and aciculate; trunk length/tergal extension length: 12.9 – 15.7 (average 14.3)%. Sieve plates small.

Key characters and similar species

The species belongs to the Echinoderes spinifurca species group thatall share the same spine/tube pattern (see above) and are characterized by long, acicular tergal extensions, i.e., tergal extensions resembling needles rather than gradually tapering triangular tips. Presence/absence of glandular cell outlets type 2 varies within the group, but if present, they would always be at segments 2, 8 and 9. Species within the group are distinguished by the absence/presence of these outlets, and by differences in trunk length/tergal extension length ratio (=TL/TE ratio).

E. yamasakiihas glandular cell outlets type 2 on segments 2, 8 and 9, which makes it differ from E. spinifurca (no outlets at all) and E. sylviae (only outlets on segments 8 and 9). The remaining species in the species group, E. lupherorum, E. kohniand E. microaperturus, have spine/tube patterns and glandular cell outlets type 2 identical with E. yamasakii, but the species is very easily distinguished from these by its very long tergal extensions, i.e., TL/TE ratio in E. yamasakii = 14.3%, opposite to 8.3% in E. kohni, 7.2% in E. lupherorum, 5.0% in E. microaperturus.
