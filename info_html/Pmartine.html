Polacanthoderes martinezi

Polacanthoderes martinezi Sørensen, 2008

Short description

ectinate fringe on segment 1 present along ventromedial and paraventral margin, and strongly serrated.
Segment 2 with midventral andlateroventral complete articulations.
Cuticular hairs and perforation sites absent on all segments. Trunk with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8 and lateroventral spines on segments 6-9; of which all lateroventral spines being similar in width.
Rather small spines in various positions on tergal plate ofsegments 4-9: usually present midlaterally on segment 5, subdorsally, laterodorsallyand lateral accessory on segments 6 and 7, laterodorsally and lateral accessory on segments 8 and 9: but rarely absent somesmall spines oradditionally present on subdorsal spines on segments 5 and 8.
Similar small spines also present ventromedial position on segments 4 -7 and ventrolateral position on segments 8-10. Tubes on midlateral position on segment 4, lateroventral position on segment 5, and laterodorsal position on segment 10; also rarely present in subdorsal position on segment 2 and/or 5.
Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in midlateral position on segments 6 and 8.
Ventromedial setae present on segment 11.
Lateral terminal spines thin and long, and females with papillae/pores in ventromedial position on segment 6 and ventrolateral position on segment 7, and lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

the Polacanthodres species areunique in having many spines on segment, and strongly serrated primary pectinate fringe only in ventromedial to paraventral margin on segment 1. Among the Polacanthoderes, P. martinezi can be distinguished from P. shiraseae in having similar-width lateroventral spines throughout the segments 6-9. In addition, P. martinezihas lateral accessory small acicular spines on segment 7, whereas P. shiraseae has sublateral small acicular spines on the same segment.

Distribution and habitat

sediment at 2274-2290 m depth in north of South Shetland Islands, Southern Atlantic Ocean.
