Short description

long middorsal spines on segments 5, 6, 8, lateroventral spines/tubes on segments 5-9 and lateroventral tubes on segment 2. Lateral terminal spines long and acicular. Tergal extensions long triangular.

Key characters and similar species

The species can be recognized by its unique pattern of spines and tubes, especially the middorsal spine patternis not shared with any other species. The description is old lacking details of the presence/absence of glandular cell outlets type 2, female papillae, etc., and therefore requires redescription.

Distribution and habitat

riginally described from Ostend, Belgium posteriorly recorded from 140 m depthin the North Sea. The details of the sediment or the coordinates are not available in the original description. Zelinka reported the species from Trieste, Italy 22 m depth and Dal Zotto and Todaro from Chioggia, Italyin coarse silt at 20-29 m depth. However, both of these reports found differences in the spine arrangements of the species when comparing with the original description.
