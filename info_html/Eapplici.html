Short description

Relatively plump species, belonging to the Echinoderes coulli species group.
Segment 11 can be completely retracted into segment 10, and segment 10 partly retracted into segment 9.
No middorsal spines present; tubes present in lateroventral positions on segments 5, in lateral accessory positions on segment8, and in laterodorsal positions on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 not present. Lateral terminal spines acicular; females without lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plates large and elongate.

Key characters and similar species

eneral lack of spines combined with the large sieve plate, easily narrows the number of potential species down to those that are part of the Echinoderes coulli species group. The combination of tubes is in itself enough to distinguish E. applicitus from other species in the group. Another helpful character is the retractable segments 10 and 11, which is only shared with two other species.

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality in the Segara Anakan Lagoon, on the south coast of Java, Indonesia, from intertidal mud flats.
The species was very common and very abundant throughout the estuary.
