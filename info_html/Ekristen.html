Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segments 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8 and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in subdorsal and lateroventral position on segment 2 (not mentioned but illustrated in the original paper). Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines long and thin. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species similar to E. higginsiand E. riedli in the spine/tube pattern and the type-2 gland cell outlets pattern. However, the species clearly differs from E. higginsiin the cigar-shaped trunk (rectangular shape in E. higginsi) and long triangular tergal extension (tergal extensions triangular with gradually extending rigid tip).
Echinoderes kristenseni differs from E. riedli at least in the length of middorsal spines (much longer in E. kristenseni than E. riedli).

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from its type locality, in the English Channel, France and mud sediment at 8 m depth from the Atlantic coast of Spain.
