Echinoderes ajax

Echinoderes ajax Sørensen, 2014

Short description

Species with very short middorsal spine on segment 4, segment 2 with tubes in subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral andventrolateral positions, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on

segment 8, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Type 2 glandular cell outlets not present. Lateral terminal spines acicular, andfemales with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is easily recognized by its spine/tube pattern that is shared only with E. capitatusand E. isabelae. However, these two species have a completely different general trunk appearance, being nearly rectangular and with a very short segment 2. Oppositely, E. ajax, has the more common cigar-shaped trunk.

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality in São Sebastião, Brazil, and was described from subtidal mud.
