Short description

Species with plump-shaped trunk, middorsal spine on segment 4, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, sublateral spines on segments 6 and 7, sublateral tubes on segment 8 and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in paradorsal, laterodorsal and ventromedial position on segment 2, lateroventral position on segments 3 and 5, subdorsal and laterodorsal position on segment 4, midlateral position on segments 6 and 7, and laterodorsal position on segment 8. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines thin acicular. Sieve plate medium in size.

Key characters and similar species

The species resembles to E. maxwelli, E. ohtsukai, E. rex, and E. teretis in the presence of middorsal spine on segment 4 and paired tubes/spines on segments 5-8. However, the species can be distinguished from E. maxwelli, E. ohtsukai, and E. rex in spines/tubes of segments 6-8 displaced laterally into sublateral position (at least one pair of those spines/tubes are in the lateroventral position in E. maxwelli, E. ohtsukai, and E. rex). Echinoderes regina and E. teretis has the identical spine pattern, however, they differs each other in (1) general trunk shape (plump shape in E. regina and broad shape in E. teretis) and (2) proportion between lateral terminal terminal spine and trunk length (lateral terminal spine length is 10-20% of trunk length in E. regina, whereas 40-70% in E. teretis).

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from its type locality, muddy sediment at 10 m depth near Hon Mieu Island, Nha Trang, Vietnam.
