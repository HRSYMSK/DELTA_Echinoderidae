Short description

Species with middorsal spine on segments 4, 6 and 8, ventrolateral tubes in segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5 and laterove ntral spines on segments 6 to 9. Additional tubes are insubdorsal tubeson segment 2, laterodorsal tubes on segments 8 and 10 andlateral accessory tubes on segments 6 to 8.
Type-2 gland cell outlets present in sublateral position on segment 2. Lateral terminal spines short and stout, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines.

Key characters and similar species

Among the Echinoderes species, E. brevipeshas unusual spine/tube pattern, only resembling to E. intermediusand E. newcaledoniensis. However, E. brevipes can be easily distinguished from the latter two species in theappearance of lateral terminal spines 2: short and stout in E. brevipes, whereas long andacicular in the latter two species..

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality, sediment at 0.7-1.0 m depth in Dominican Republic, Caribbean Sea.
