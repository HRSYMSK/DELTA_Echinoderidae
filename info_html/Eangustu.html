Echinoderes angustus

Echinoderes angustus Higgins & Kristensen, 1988

Short description

Slender species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 5, laterove ntral spines on segments 6 to 9. Glandular cell outlets type 2 (not shown in original description) present in subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral and ventrolateral positions on segment 2, subdorsal positions on segment 4, midlateral positions on segment 5, sublateral positions on segment 8 (large ones), and laterodorsal positions on segment 10. Lateral terminal spines acicular, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plates small. A weak indication of a partial midsternal fissure is visible on segment 2.

Key characters and similar species

The species can relatively easily be recognized by the combined patterns of spines, tubes and glandular cell outlets type 2.
E. angustus shows closest resemblance with E. pennaki. The two species can be distinguished by the glandular cell outlets type in laterodorsal positions on segment 10, present in E. angustusbut absent in E. pennaki. These glands can be difficult to see though. Another indicative difference regards the length of the middorsal and laterodorsal spines that generally are longer in E. angustus. E. angustus also resembles E. remanei, but this species can be distinguished from most others by its very strong and broad ventral pectinate fringe on segment 1 (note that occurrence of glandular cell outlets type 2 is unknown for E. remanei, hence the species could also differ at this point). This fringe is less differentiated and conspicuous in E. angustus.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from subtidal mud around Disko Island, West Greenland, but has subsequently been reported from Svalbard.
