Short description

pecies with incomplete tergosternal divisions on segment 2. Longmiddorsal spines on segments 4, 6, 8, segment 2 with tubes in ventrolateral position, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8 and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Without glandular cell outlets type 2. Lateral terminal spines very long (exceeding the trunk length), and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small. Tergal extensions enlarging gradually into long acicular tips.

Key characters and similar species

The species with more resemblance are M. macracanthusand M. herranzae but both species can be distinguished form M. boylei based on the short tergal extensionsand the presence of lateral accessory tubes on s.8. Additionally, M. herranzaehas laterodorsal tubes on segment 2.

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality 3 miles off Fort Pierce, Florida collected from muddy sand at 11 m depth.
