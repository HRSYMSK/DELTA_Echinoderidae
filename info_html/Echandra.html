Short description

E. dujardinii species group, and has middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segments 2 and 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10; middorsal spines may reach, but never extend beyond the posterior segment margin.
Midlateral glandular cell outlets type 2 present on segments 8 and 9.

The species has the short, well-spaced triangular tergal extensions that are typical for E. dujardinii species group members. Lateral terminal spines are acicular, and females have lateral terminal accessory spines, and female papillaein ventrolateral positions on segments 6 and 7, and in ventromedial positions on segment 8; intracuticular substructuresof female papillaeon all segments are crescentic with a small protuberance in the curved part of the structure.

Key characters and similar species

Distinguishing species within the E. dujardinii species group can be really challenging. Species of the group are generally characterised by glandular cell outlets type 2 in laterodorsal or midlateral positons on segments 8 and/or 9, sternal female papillae on certain segments from 6 to 8, short, well-spaced triangular tergal extensions, and the same spine pattern: middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segments 2 and 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and laterodorsal spines on segment 10. The only variation regards lateral accessory spines on segment 8, that are present in E. dujardiniiand E. gerardi only. One of the important keys to identity some of the species seems to be the relative length of the middorsal spines.
