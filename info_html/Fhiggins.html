Short description

Segment 2 consists of one tergal and two sternal plate divided bylateroventral and midventral articulations.
Trunk with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, laterodorsal and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6, 7 and 9, midlateral spine on segment 8, and midlateral tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in laterodorsal position on segments 5, 6, 8 and 9. Lateral terminal spines thin and long, and females withventromedial pores/papillaeon segment 7 andlateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is easily recognized by the presence of middorsal spines on segments 4-8, laterodorsal and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, and midlateral spines on segment 8.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from at 40- 885 m depth around New Zealand, at 3675-3853 m depth off California, USA, and at 41150 m depth in thepolymetallic nodule fields at the Clarion-Clipperton Fracture Zone in East Pacific Ocean.
