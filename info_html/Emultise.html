Short description

Species without any middorsal spines, with ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segments 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, ventromedial tubes on segment 9 and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10.
Glandular cell outlets type 2 unknown. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines long and thin. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species similar to E. horni, E. malakhovi and E. parahorni in the absence of any middorsal spines and the presence of lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9. However, E.
multisetosus can be recognized in the presence oflateral accessory tubes on segments 7 and 8, and ventromedial tubes on segment 9.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known muddy fine sand and mud at 5 – 10 m depth from Peter the Great Bay ( type locality), Russia.
