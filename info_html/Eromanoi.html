Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, and lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present on segment 2 in subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral and ventromedial positions, on segment 5 in midlateral positions, and on segment 8 in sublateral positions. Tergal extensions arerelatively long, broad and triangular. Lateral terminal spines are long, acicular and thin, whereas female lateral terminal accessory spines are rather short. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species can be recognized by its combined spine/tube pattern and distribution of glandular cell outlets type 2. With all spines, tubes and outlets taken into account, E. brevicaudatusmay appear as an alternative identity, but this is only because thedistribution (or eventual presence) of glandular cell outlets type 2 in this species still needs to be examined. However, with its very short spines, and in particular its short and stout lateral terminal spines, it cannot in any way be confused with E. romanoi.

Distribution and habitat

The species lives in subtidal mud along the shelf of the northeastern Gulf of Mexico, from Louisiana to the Florida Panhandle.
Its western distribution seems to be limited by the outlet of Mississippi River.
