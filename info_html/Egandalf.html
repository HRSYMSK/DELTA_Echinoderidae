Key characters and similar species

The spine pattern with middorsal spines on segments 6 and 8 is rare among Echinoderes species, and is shared with only three species, i.e., E. daenerysae, E. hviidarumand E. ultraabyssalis, and an undescribed species, Echinoderes sp. 1, from the Atacama Trench (Grzelaket al. 2021). Furthermore, E. gandalfi possesses laterodorsal tubes on segment 9, which isanother relatively rare trait, shared with all abovementioned species, which suggests that these species might represent a group of closely related species.

Nevertheless, despite the overall similarity, E. gandalfisp. nov. can be distinguished from these congeners by its morphometrics.
Echinoderes gandalfiis markedly smaller in trunk length than these species but has proportionally longer lateral terminal spines, which always exceed the trunk length, resulting in markedly higher TL/LTS ratio in E. gandalfisp. nov. in comparison to E. ultraabyssalis and Echinoderessp. 1 (TL/LTS: 132% vs. 59% and 91%, respectively). It appears that also the arrangement and number of sensory spots show some differences. Unique for E. gandalfi sp. nov. isthe presence of laterodorsal sensory spots on segment 2 but thelack of these structures in ventrolateral positions on segment 1, and in middorsal position on segment 10 as found in the other two species. In addition, E. ultraabyssalis lacks of well-developed laterodorsal tubes on segment 10, which are presentin both sexes and easy to visualize even with LM in E. gandalfisp. nov.

Distribution and habitat

Hikurangi Margin off New Zealand, where it has been recorded from Pahaua- and Honeycomb Canyon, on depths from 730 to 1 495 m.
