Short description

Species without middorsal spine, with lateroventral tubes on segments5 and 8 and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 unknown. Lateral terminal spines thin long, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate large.

Key characters and similar species

The absence of middorsal spines and lateroventral spine as well as the presence of lateroventral tubes on segments 5 and 8 suggest the species belongs or is closely related to the Echinoderes coulli species group. Among the all Echinoderes species, E. filispinosus resembles to E. bengalensis and E. komatsui in spine/tube pattern. However, E. filispinosus differs from E. bengalensis in general trunk shape (plum shape in E. filispinosus, whereas cigar shape in E. bengalensis) and relative length of lateral terminal spine (50– ?60% of trunk length in E. filispinosus, whereas 10– ?30% of trunk length in E. bengalensis). Echinoderes filispinosus can be distinguished from E. komatsui in the shape of tergal extension shape (long triangular in E. filispinosus, whereas short triangular in E. komatsui). In addition, E. filispinosus has slightly longer lateral terminal spines (50– ?60% of trunk length in E. filispinosus, whereas 30– ?50% of trunk length in E. komatsui).

Distribution and habitat

The species is recorded only from its type locality, muddy fine sand at 5-10 m depth in the Peter the Great Bay, Sea of Japan, Russia.
