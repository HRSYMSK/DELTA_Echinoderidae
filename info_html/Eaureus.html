Echinoderes aureus

Echinoderes aureus Adrianov, Murakami & Shirayama, 2002

Short description

Cigar-shaped species with indication of intracuticular midventral fissure on segment 2. The species has middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral/ventrolateral tubes on segments 2 and 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and midlateral tubes on segment 10.
Type-2 gland cell outlets absent. Middorsal spines arealways extending beyond the posterior segment margin. The species has the short, well-spaced triangular tergal extensions, that aresimilar to that of E. dujardinii species group members. Lateral terminal spines are acicular, and females have lateral terminal accessory spines andventrolateral papillae on segment 7 and ventromedial papillae on segment 8. Sieve plate small.

However, it can be distinguished from the group members in the dorsal pattern of the gland cell outlet type 1: E. aureushas them middorsally on segments 1 2, 3, 5 and 7 and paradorsally on segments 4, 6, 8 and 9, whereas the members of E. dujardinii have the features middorsally on segments 1, 2, 3 and 10 and subdorsally on segments 4-9.
In addition, the presence of its rather distinct intracuticular fissure midventrally on segment 2 is relatively rarecharacter in Echinoderidae, and thus, helps identify the species.

Note that Echinoderes lanceolatuswas synonymized to this species in Sørensen et al. (2020).

Distribution and habitat

The species is described from Seto in the Japan Inland Sea, but there are also several records of the species from the Boso peninsula and Tanegashima Island (Japan)
as well as Jeju Island (Korea).
