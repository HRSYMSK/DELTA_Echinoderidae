Short description

pecies with middorsal spines on segments 6, and 8, tubes on segment 2 in laterodorsal and ventrolateral positions (ventrolateral tubes might be missing in some specimens), tubes on segment 5 in lateroventral positions, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, laterodorsal tubes on segment 9, and very short laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 not present. Lateral terminal spines long and acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines; malemorphology unknown. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The presence of middorsal spines on segments 6 and 8 only is a quite rare combination and only shared with the three deep-sea species E. hviidarum, E. ultraabyssalis, and Meristoderes okhotensis. Resemblance with the latter is only superficial though, and the extremely long tergal extensions of M. okhotensis easily distinguish it from the three Echinoderes species. These three species are much more similar though, but E. daenerysaecan be distinguished from them by its presence of laterodorsal tubes on segment 2.

Distribution and habitat

was described from Hornsund Fjord on Spitsbergen, Svalbard.
It has been found in fjords as well as open waters around Svalbard, but appears to be most abundant in the fjords. Even though a total of 34 individuals have been identified from Svalbard, not a single male has been found so far.
