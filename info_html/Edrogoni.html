Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateral accessory tubes on segment 5, and lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present on segment 2 in subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral and ventromedial

positions, on segment 5 in midlateral positions, on segment 8 in sublateral positions, and on segment 10 in laterodorsal positions. Tergal extensions are probably sexually dimorphic, and appear to be shorter and more rounded in females, while they are slightly longer and triangularin males; in both sexes they extend into long, flexible tips.
The terminal segment also appears to have a middorsal fissure in its tergal plate, which is partly covered by a protuberance. Lateral terminal spines long, acicular and thin; females witheven thinner lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The split of the terminal segment into two tergal plates is quite uncommon among specie of Echinoderes, but the character is often difficult to see, to the easiest way to distinguish the species is by the combined spines, tube and glandular cell outlet type 2 patterns which in combination are unique. The relocation of the lateral accessory tubes on segment 5 (opposed to the more common ventrolateral tubes) quickly narrows the number of potential species down to very few, and also the presence of glandular cell outlets type 2 in ventromedial positions is rather uncommon.

Distribution and habitat

The species is so far only known from the waters around Svalbard. It was described from Hornsund Fjord on Spitsbergen, Svalbard, but it also occurs in other Svalbard fjords at in the open waters around the islands. Even though it might not be that abundant, it appears to be one of the most common species around Svalbard.
