Short description

Species with nearly rectangular trunk shape, and middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9 (note tha

t lateroventral tubes on segment 5 is missing), and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in midlateral positions on segment 1, subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral and ventrolateral positions on segment 2, lateral accessory positions on segment 5, and sublateral positions on segment 8. Lateral terminal spines acicular and very long – same as trunk length; female dimorphic morphology unknown. Sieve plates small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is very easily recognized by its unique combination of spine and glandular cell outlets type 2 patterns. Especially the presence of midlateral glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment 1 is very uncommon, and only shared with two other deep-sea species – E. hamiltonorumand E. unispinosus. However, these three species differ in many other ways (e.g., E. hamiltonorum has a pair of tubes and no glandular cell outlets on segment 2, and E. unispinosus only a single middorsal spine), and there is no way they can be confused.

Distribution and habitat

E. anniaeis a deep-sea species, known from 3250 m depth off Oregon at the US west coast.
