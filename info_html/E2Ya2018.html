Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8 andlaterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in laterodorsal position on segment 2. Lateral terminal spines thin and long, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

Although the spine/tube pattern is unique among the all known Echinoderes species, the species has not beennamed because only a single deteriorated specimen available so far.

Distribution and habitat

The species was recorded fromthe deep-sea plain near the Sedlo Seamount, northeastern Atlantic Ocean, at 2875 m depth.
