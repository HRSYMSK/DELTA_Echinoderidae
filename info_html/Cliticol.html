Key characters and similar species

e species is relatively easily distinguished from my species, inclusive congeners, by its minute size and strong lateral compression of its trunk. The only other species showing close resemblance is C. flosculosa, which also happens to share habitat with C. liticola. The two species can only be distinguished by the small midventral floscula, apparently present in C. flosculosa

only, and by minor differences in sensory spot distribution, inclusive presence paradorsal sensory spots on segment 9 and lack of paradorsal sensory spots on segment 10, characteristic for C. flosculosa, opposite to lack paradorsal sensory spots on segment 9 and presence of paradorsal sensory spots on segment 10, characteristic for C. liticola. Furthermore, C. flosculosahas subdorsal sensory spots on segments 9 and 10; such sensory spots are missing in C. liticola.

Distribution and habitat

The species is described from intertidal localities, ca. 70 deep holes around mid-tide level, in asandy beachon the northeast coast of Oahu, Hawaii. The beach was rather narrow, and located next to the coastal Kamehameha Highway (Highway 83). When the type locality was re-visited in 2017 the beach had been replaced with large boulders to secure the road from erosion, hence, the type locality doesnot exist any longer.
