Exalkuta

Echinoderes xalkutaat Cepeda, Á lvarez-Castillo, Hermoso-Salazar, Sánchez, Gómez & Pardos, 2019

Species with middorsal spineson segments 4 -8, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6-9. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present insubdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral and ventrolatral position on segment 2, midlateral position onsegment 5, sublateral position on segment 8, laterodorsal position on segment 10.
Lateral terminal spines long acicular. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species has the resemble spine/ tube pattern to those in E. drogoni, E. remaneiand E. romanoi. Among them, E. xalkutaat can be easily distinguished from E. remaneiin having thin pectinate fringe teeth on segment 1.
Echinoderes xalkutaat differs also from E. drogoni in having lateroventral tubes on segment 5, which are displaced laterally into the lateral accessory position in E. drogoni; from E. romanoiin having the laterodorsal type-2 gland cell outlets on segment 10.

Distribution and habitat

The species isfound from mud sediment at 1440 m depthin the Gulf of California.
