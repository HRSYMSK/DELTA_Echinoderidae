Echinoderes veracruzensis

Echinoderes veracruzensis Anguas-Escalante et al., 20 25

Short description

l Echinoderes with acicular

spines in middorsal position on segments 4, 6, and 8, with spine on segment 8 being up to three times longer than spine on segment 4. Additional spines present in lateroventral positions on segments 7 to segment 9, tubes present in lateroventral positions

on segment 2 and 5. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in subdorsal positions on segment 2, midlateral positions on segment 7 and 8, and in laterodorsal positions on segment 10; outlets on segment 10 with short, broad, projecting rectangular flap. Male morphology unknown.

Key characters and similar species

E. veracruzensishas only lateroventral spines on segments 7 to 9, while they are lacking on segment 6, and this condition is only shared with E. gama and E.
riceae. Of these two species, E. gamais easily distinguished from the new species by having five middorsal spines, and no tubes or glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment 2.
E.
riceaeis more similar to the newspecies, but it also differs at several points, inclusive its lack of glandular cell outlets type 2 at any segment, and the presence of lateral accessory tubes on segment 8 (Herranz et al. 2014). In addition, E. veracruzensis sp. nov. has very conspicuous tergal extensions that differ from most other species by its long and pointed tips.

E. veracruzensisis also one of the smallest among the Echinoderes, with a trunk length of 146– 160.
