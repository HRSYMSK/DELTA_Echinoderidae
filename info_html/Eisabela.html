Short description

Species withmiddorsal spine on segment 4, segment 2 with subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral and ventrolateral tubes, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6-9. Additional tubes inmidlateral position on segment 7, and subdorsal and lateral accessory position on segment 8. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Lateral terminal spines long and thin, and females without lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is the most similar to E. capitatus in having middorsal spine only on segment 4, lateroventral spines on segment 6-9, and rectangular-shaped trunk with strong and well-developed pachycycli. The two species are differentiated by the presence/absence of the laterodorsal tubes on segment 10 and sensory spot pattern. However, a future study of comprehensive data on E. isabelae  and  E. capitatus may or may not suggest the differences between them are intraspecific variation of a same species (See the discussion in Yamasaki & Dal Zotto 2019 in Zool. Anz.).

Distribution and habitat

The species is known fine sand to muddy sand around the Iberian Peninsula.
