Short description

Species withmiddorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, andlateroventral spines on segments 6-9. Tubesin subdorsal, sublateral and ventrolateral positionon segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segments5, midlateral andlateral accessory position on segment 8 and laterodorsal position on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent.
Lateral terminal spines thin long, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

Among the species with the middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8. E.
hispanicus resembles to E. balerioni, E. newcaledoniensis and E. peterseniin having three pairs of tubes on segment 2. However, E. hispanicuscan be distinguished from E. balerioniand E. peterseniin having additional pair of tubes in midlateral position on segment 8. Echinoderes hispanicus differs from E.
newcaledoniensis inlacking the lateral accessory tubes on segments 6 and 8 and the sublateral tubes on segment 9.

Distribution and habitat

The species is recorded fromsediment of coarse/fine sand, shell gravel, muddy sand, mud at 3- 45 m depth around the Iberian Peninsula.
