Short description

Slender species with a single middorsal spine on segment 4, lateroventral tubes on segments 2 and 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and lateral accessory tubes on segment 8.
Laterodorsal tubes are also present on segment 10, but these are so small that they easily can be overlooked. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present on segment 2 in subdorsal and sublateral positions. Lateral terminal spines long and acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

can be distinguished by its spine pattern combined with the distribution of glandular cell outlets type 2.
This combined pattern is only similar with one other species, Meristoderes galatheae. However, except for the partial plate differentiation in segment 2 of M. galatheae, E. charlotteaeis distinguished by its way longer lateral terminal spines, and presence of a middorsal protuberance between segments 10 and 11.

Distribution and habitat

The species lives in subtidal mud in the northeastern Gulf of Mexico, from Louisiana and east towards Florida.
