Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, laterodorsal tubes on segment 7, and subdorsal tubes on segment 9. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Lateral terminal spines long and thin. Female withlateral terminal accessory spines.

Key characters and similar species

The species sharesthe spine/tube pattern and the absence of the type-2 gland cell outlet with E. kaempfae, E.
pterus and E. remanei.
Echinoderes stockman differs from E. kaempfaein the shape of the tergal extension (long triangular in E. stockman, whereas tergal extension gradually extending into thin rigid tip in E. kaempfae), is no longer confused with other congeners based on its spine/tube pattern, especially the presence of the laterodorsal tubes on segment 7 as well as the subdorsal tubes on segment 9.
the absence of hairless segment 1, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Echinoderes stockman can be easily distinguished from E. pterusin the absence of thetufts of cuticular hairs in laterodorsal position on segment 9. Echinoderes stockman differs from E. remaneiin thelonger lateral terminal spine (80-90% of trunk length in E. stockman, whereas 50-60% of trunl length in E. remanei), and the pectinate fringe on segment 1 being similar in shape to thaton segment 2 in E. stockman (pectinate fringe teeth on segment 1 being conspicuously broader than those on segment 2 in E. remanei).

Distribution and habitat

The species is known fromits type locality, the muddy sedimentin Stockman oil field, Barents Sea.
