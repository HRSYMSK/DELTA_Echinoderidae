Short description

Echinoderes with spines in middorsal position on segments 4 to 8, and in lateroventral positions on segments 6 to 9; middorsal ones

segments 4-8, each extending beyond the primary pectinate fringe of their respective segment; middorsal spine on segment 8 also extending beyond the primary pectinate fringe of segment 9. Tubes present in lateroventral positions on segment 5. Glandular cell outlets type 2 in

subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral and ventrolateral positions onsegment 2, subdorsal positions on segment 4, midlateral positions on

segment 5, sublateral positions on segment 8, and laterodorsal positionson segment 10. Lateral terminal spines short and stout, ranging from 16

% to 21 % of the trunk length.
Segment 11 composed oftwo tergal and two sternal plates.

Key characters and similar species

The presence of short, stout lateral terminal spines, combined with spine and glandular cell outlet patterns, almost make the species unique, and it can only be confused with E. obtuspinosus.
Nevertheless, E. quasaecan be easily distinguished from

E. obtuspinosus by using morphometric differences. Next to the bigger

trunk size for E. obtuspinosus (range of TL = 341- µm vs. 297 µm, respectively), the most conspicuous difference between thespecies is the length of lateral terminal spines, which are longer in

E. quasae (range of LTS = 52 -74 µmvs 46- 53 µ m, respectively), resulting in higher LTS/TL ratio in this species compared to

E. obtuspinosus (range of LTS/TL: 16– 21 % vs 14– 15 %, respectively).
Middorsal, as well as lateroventral spines, are also significantly longerin E. quasae compared with E. obtuspinosus.
