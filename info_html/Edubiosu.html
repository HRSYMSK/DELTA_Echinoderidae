Short description

Slender species with very long lateral terminal spines, often exceeding the trunk length. Middorsal spines present on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, sublateral tubes

on segment 8, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 only present in midlateral positions on segment 2. The lateroventral spines on segment 9 are conspicuously thick and extend beyond the margin of the terminal segment. Lateral terminal spines acicular and very long; female dimorphic morphology unknown. Sieve plates small.

Key characters and similar species

The key character to easily recognize this species is the long and stout lateroventral spines on segment 9 that extend beyond the margin of the terminal segment. This character is only shared with E. levanderi, E. pterus, and E. bathyalis. However, two of these species cannot be confused with E. dubiosus.
E. levanderiis a brackish water species with stout trunk, and very large sieve plates, whereas E. pterus has its conspicuous subdorsal fringes on segment 9.
The species that shows most resemblance with E. dubiosusis the Atlantic deep-sea species E. bathyalis, that is so similar that the authors describing E. dubiosus were uncertain whether it represented a distinct species or a Pacific population of E. bathyalis – hence, the name dubiosus. For now it seems like the two species can be distinguished by: 1) the middorsal spine on segment 10 not extending beyond the terminal segment in E. dubiosus, 2) the tubes on segment 5 being lateroventral in E. dubiosus, but displaced to lateral accessory positions in E. bathyalis, 3) the presence of sublateral tubes on segment 8 in E. dubiosus, opposite to no tubes, but perhaps lateral accessory glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment 8 in E. bathyalis, 4) the presence of laterodorsal tubes on segment 10 in E. dubiosus only, and finally 4) a few differences in distribution of sensory spots.
