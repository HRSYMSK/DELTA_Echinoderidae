Short description

three middorsal spines on segments 4, 6, 8, lateroventral spines onsegments 6 – 9; tubes present on subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral, and ventrolateral positions on segment 2, lateroventral position on segment 5, and sublateral positionon segment 8. Males with well-developed tubesin laterodorsal position on the posterior margin of segment 10.
Type 2 glandular cell outlets not present.
Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

of the key characters fordifferentiating this species from other similar onesis the general trunk appearance being nearly rectangular and with a very short segment 2. The general shape combined with the presence of 3 middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 , 8 and 4 pairs of tubes on segment 2 is unique for E. hakaiensis.
Echinoderes peterseni shares a nearly identical tube/spine formula with E. hakaiensisbut lacks the laterodorsal pairof tubes on segment 2 and the tubes on segment 8 are in the lateral accessory position; it has significantly longer lateral terminal spines compared with E. hakaiensis and the trunk shape is not rectangular but cigar-shaped.

Distribution and habitat

several localities in the Central coast of British Columbia, Canada collected from sandy mud atdepths ranging from 88-140 m; also recorded inthe continental rise off California coast from mud at 2719 mdepth.
