Short description

Species with conspicuously long, gradually tapering tergal extensions, and middorsal spines on segments 4, 6, and 8, tubeson segment 2 in laterodorsal, sublateral and ventrolateral positions, tubes on segment 5 in lateroventral positions, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and lateral accessory tubes on segment 8. Glandular cell outlets type 2 not present. Lateral terminal spines acicular; female morphology unknown. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

very long tergal extensions, combined with the spine/tube pattern makes it very easy to distinguish the species from all others.

Distribution and habitat

is only known from its type locality at 880 m depth, north of Svalbard.
