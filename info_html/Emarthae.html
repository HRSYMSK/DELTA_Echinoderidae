Short description

Relatively plump species, belonging to the Echinoderes coulli

species group. Spines are only present on the terminal segment. Tubes are present in lateroventral positions of segments 5 and 8, and in laterodorsal positions on segment 8. Males furthermore with thin laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Tergal extensions form a pair of rather narrow tips, often extending into fringes that resemble seta-like tips. Lateral terminal spines are acicular and relatively short, and lateral terminal accessory spines arelacking in females. Sieve plates large and elongate.

Key characters and similar species

The general lack of spines (except for lateral terminal ones) combined with the large sieve plate, easily narrows the number of potential species down to those that are part of the Echinoderes coulli species group. Among these species, the spine/tube pattern of E. marthaeis in itself enough to distinguish the species.
The distribution of tubes in E. marthaeis close to the pattern in the Panamanian species E. strii, but they can be distinguished by the midlateral tubes on segment 3, present in E. strii only. This species is also, opposite to E. marthae, able to retract segments 10 and 11 into segment 9.

Distribution and habitat

was described from intertidal mudflats in Araçá Bay, São Sebastiã o, Brazil, and is also known from similar mudflats at the island Ilhabela that is separated from the type locality be the São Sebastião channel. The species has furthermore been found in high numbers inaround Papagayo Island in the Gulf of Paranaguá, Brazil.
