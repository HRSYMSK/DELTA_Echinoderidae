Short description

Species withmiddorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6-9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Lateral terminal spines long and thin, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species shares the spine/tube pattern, the long thin lateral terminal spines and the absenceof any type-2 gland cell outlets with E. pterus, E. remanei, and E. stockmani. However, E. kaempfae can be easily distinguished from E. pterusin the absence of the tufts of long cuticular hairsin laterodorsal position on segment 9, and the shape of the tergal extension ( triangular with gradually extending rigid tip in E. kaempfae, whereas truncated or not well-defined tergal extension in E. pterus).
Echinoderes kaempfae differs from E. remanei and E. stockman also in the shape of tergal extension (that in the latter two species is long triangular). In addition, E. kaempfaecan be distinguished from E. remaneiin having longer lateral terminal spines ( longer than 80% of the trunk length) than those in E. remanei (50-60% of trunk length), and the primary pectinate fringe teethon segment 1 not being conspicuously wider than those on the preceding segments.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from aroundits type locality, on the slope to foot of the Senghor Seamount, at 1545 – 3110 m depth.
