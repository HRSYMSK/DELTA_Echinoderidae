Echinoderes okiensis

Echinoderes okiensis Yamasaki, 20 2 4

Short description

Echinoderes with spines in middorsal position on segments 4 to 8, and in lateroventral positions on segments 6 to 9; of which middorsal oneon segment 4 reaching the primary pectinate fringe of the corresponding segment, whereas those on segments 5 -8, each extending beyond the primary pectinate fringe of their respective segment. Tubes present inventrolateral position on segment 2, lateroventral

position on segment 5, and midlateral position on segment 10.
Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent.
Glandular cell outlets type 1 present middorsally eachon segments 1– 3, 5, and 7, two present middorsally on segments 10 and

11, one pairs present paradorsally on segments 4, 6, 8, and 9, sublaterallyon segment 1, and ventromedially on segments 2– 10. Female papillae with tubular-shaped intracuticular substructure present in ventrolateral position on segment 6 and ventromedial position on segment 7. Lateral terminal spines thin and long, ranging from 41 % to 59 % of the trunk length.
Tergal extension narrowing abruptly into

long acicular tips, constituting 5– 7% of trunk length.

Key characters and similar species

Based on the arrangement of type-1 glandular cell outlets and the shape of the tergal extensions, morphologically similar species are restricted to Echinoderes aureus (which shares the same pattern of type- 1 glandular cell outlets) and members of the Echinoderes spinifurca group (which exhibit similar tergal extension shapes). However, no species exhibiting both the type- 1 glandular cell outlet pattern and the tergal extension shape of the present species have been identified to date, enabling it to be readily distinguished from its congeners by these two morphological features.
