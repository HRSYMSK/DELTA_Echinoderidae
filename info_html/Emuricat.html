Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8 andlaterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines long and thin. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The presence of the middorsal spines on segments 4-8, lateroventral tubes on segment 5 andlateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9 are one of the most common pattern in Echinoderes, shared with 27 species including E. muricatus, the species can be recognized withthe presence/absence of the additional tubes and the absence of the type-2 gland cell outlets: the absence of any dorsal tubes on segment 2, the presence of lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, and the absence of the type-2 gland cell outlets on any segment.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from the Pacific coast of Panama, mud with coarse sand at intertidal zone (type locality).
