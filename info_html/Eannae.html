Short description

Relatively plump species, belonging to the Echinoderes coulli species group. Middorsal spine on segment 4 only; tubes in lateroventral positions on segments 5 and 8, in midlateral positions on segment 9, and laterodorsal positions on segment 10. Numerous minute glandular cell outlets type 2 present on several segments: subdorsal, laterodorsal and ventrolateral positions on segment 2, subdorsal positions on segment 4, sublateral positions on segment 5, laterodorsal positions on segment 6, sublateral positions on segment 7, and laterodorsal positions on segment 8. Lateral terminal spines short and stout, and females withvery short lateral terminal accessory spines that might be difficult to see. Sieve plates large and elongate.

Key characters and similar species

The general lack of spines (only middorsal on segment 4 and terminal spines) combined with the large sieve plate, easily narrows the number of potential species down to those that are part of the Echinoderes coulli species group. Among these species, the spine/tube pattern of E. annaeis in itself enough to distinguish the species.

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality at Seringat Island, south of Singapore, from intertidal sea grass.
