Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 6, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9 and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in laterodorsal position onsegments 8 and 9 (not mentioned in the original paper). Females with pore/papillae in ventromedial position on segments 7 and 8 (not mentioned in the original paper), andlateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines long and thin. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

Thespecies can be easily recognized with the presence of the middorsal spines only on segments 4-6. Any other echinoderid species has this middorsal spine pattern.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from thenorthern coast of Spain, Atlantic Ocean (type locality).
