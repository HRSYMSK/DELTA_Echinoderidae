Short description

Meristoderes species with tergosternal junctions in segment 2 appearing as indistinct lines on the cuticular surface (most easily visualized in SEM), and as lateroventral/ventrolateral interruptions in the pachycyclus (visualized in LM). Middorsal spines are present on segments 4, 6, and 8 (spine on segment 8 nearly reaching beyond terminal segment), ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, and lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9. Laterodorsal tubes also present on segment 10, emerging through slit-like openings medially on segment, and not at posterior segment margin as in most other species. Quite large glandular cell outlets type 2 are present in subdorsal and sublateral positions on segment 2, midlateral positions on segment 6, and in sublateral positions on segment 8. Segment 1 has no cuticular hairs (except around sensory spots), and tergal extensions are shortand triangular, with fringed tips. Lateral terminal spines acicular; females with rather short and thin lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species can be recognized by its combined spine/tube pattern and distribution of glandular cell outlets type 2. Especially the presence of glandular cell outlets type 2 in midlateral positions of segment 6 is quite uncommon, compared to the much more common midlateral outlets on segment 5. When keying the species, it might appear to be close to E. bermudensis

, but this is mainly because it remains uncertain whether this species has glandular cell outlets type 2, and hence is coded as uncertain for this character. The species are not similar though, and for instance, the tergal extensions in E. bermudensisare way longer and stronger than those in M. elleae.
Meristoderes elleae is instead much more similar with E. joyceae, and besides its partially developed tergosternal fissures on segment 2, the only other prominent difference between the species is the presence of sublateral glandular cell outlets type 2 present in M. elleaeand absent in E. joyceae.

Distribution and habitat

at 109-126 m depth in subtidal mud in the Korea Strait.
