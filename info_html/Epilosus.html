Short description

Species with midventral partial articulationon segment 2 (not mentioned in the previous studies). Middorsal spines presenton segments 4 to 8, each of them extending beyond the primary pectinate fringe areaon the corresponding segment.
Additional tubes/spines are lateroventral tubes on segments 2 and 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, andlaterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in subdorsal position on segment 2, and laterodorsal position on segments 8 and 9 (not mentioned in the previous studies). Females with lateral terminal accessory spines, and female papillae with tubular intracuticular substructure in ventrolateral position on segments 6 and 7 as well as in ventromedial position on segment 8 (not mentioned in the previous studies). Lateral terminal spines long and thin.
Tergal extension is short triangular.

Key characters and similar species

The species share the same spine/tube- and type-2 gland-cell-outlet- pattern with E. kohni, E. lupherorum, E. microaperturus, and E. yamasakii. However, E. pilosus can be easily distinguished from them in the shape of tergal extensions: E. pilosushas short tergal extensions, whereas the others have relatively long ones (with gradually extending tips or abruptly narrowing long tips).

Distribution and habitat

The species is known fromits type locality, landed old kelp in Grytviken, South Georgia and the South Sandwich Islands; and from intertidal and subtidal algae in Puerto Deseado, Argentina.
