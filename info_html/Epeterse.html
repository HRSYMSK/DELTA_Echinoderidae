Short description

Slender species with middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, tubes in subdorsal, sublateral and ventrolateral positions on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Tergal exten sions are triangular. Lateral terminal spines acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plates small.

Key characters and similar species

The distribution of spines and tubes in E. peterseniis unique, which makes it easy to obtain a correct identity using the key. The key might suggest E. kristensenias an alternative identity, but this is only because it remains uncertain whether this species has additional tubes on segment 2. However, it is confirmed that E. kristensenihas glandular cell outlets type 2 in subdorsal and sublateral positions on segment 2, and since such outlets are not present on any segment in E. peterseni, this character trait can be used to distinguish the species. E. peterseni also shows great resemblance with E. hispanicus, but besides have the same tubes and spines as E. peterseni, this species also has a pair of midlateral tubes on segment 8.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from Disko Island, West Greenland, where it was found inside the encrusting red algae Lithothamnium. Subsequentlyit was found in Lithothamniumin Ikka Fjord, South Greenland, and calcareous sand and gravel at Faroe Bank in the North Atlantic, which seemed to support the species preference for calcareous substrates. Later though, it was also found in regular subtidal mud at fjord and open water stations at Svalbardas well as several stations north of Svalbard, suggesting that the species is more opportunistic in its sediment preferences.
