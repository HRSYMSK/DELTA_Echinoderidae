Short description

Species with short middorsal spineson segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and lateral accessory tubes on segment 8. Type-2 glandular cell outlets present in subdorsal position on segment 2 and midlateral position on segments 4 and 5.
Primary pectinate fringe on ventral side on segment 2 much wider and longer than the other pectinate fringe, pointing toward midventral line. Sieve plate large.
Lateral terminal spines short and stout, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines.

Key characters and similar species

E. brevicaidatus is never confused with other congeners in the combination of the spine pattern, presence of large sieve plate as well as very developed primary pectinate fringe on segment 2.

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality, sandy coral mud at 30 m depthin the area of Al-Ghardaqa, Egypt, Red Sea.
