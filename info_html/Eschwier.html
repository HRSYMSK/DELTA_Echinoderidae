Short description

Species withmiddorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, lateroventral tubes on segments 2 and 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6-9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present insubdorsal position on segments 2 and 4, laterodorsal position on segments 5, 7-9.
Lateral terminal spines long and thin.
Females withpore/papillae in ventrolateral position on segment 7 and ventromedial position on segment 8, andlateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species shares the spine/tube pattern with E. apex, E. bermudensis, E. hamiltonorum, E. joyceae, and E. multiporus. However, E. schwieringaecan be discriminated from these species pattern of the type-2 gland cell outlets.
Especially, the presence of the type-2 gland cell outlets on segments 4, 5, 7 and 9 is only shared by E. schwieringaeand E. multiporus among the above species.
Echinoderes schwieringaeand E. multiporusare resemble each other in having many type-2 gland cell outlets on dorsal side, however, E.
schwieringae differs from E. multiporus in the position of the type-2 gland cell outlets on segment 4 (subdorsal in E. schwieringae, whereas laterodorsal in E.
multiporus), absence of the type2 gland cell outlets on segment 6, and the presence of the female pore/papillae on segment 7.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from its type locality, on thesummit of the Senghor Seamount, at 132 m depth.
