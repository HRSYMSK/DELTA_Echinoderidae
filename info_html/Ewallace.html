Short description

Slender species with middorsal spines on segments 4, 6, 8, segment 2 with tubes in ventrolateral positions, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, andsublateral tubes on segment 8. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in subdorsal and lateroventral positions on segment 2 (not shown in original description). Lateral terminal spines long and acicular, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species has a relatively common distribution of spines and tubes on segments 2 to 9, and it is shared with 5 other species.
Among these species, the glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment 2 helps recognize E.
wallaceae. When taking these into account (in combination with spine/tube patterns), it can only be confused with E. kristenseni.
E. wallaceae and E. kristenseniare indeed very similar, and diagnostic differences come down to small differences in spine dimensions and trunk widths.

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality at Carrie Bow Cay, Belize, from mangrove mud with seagrass at 1-3 m depth.
