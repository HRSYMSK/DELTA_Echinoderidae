Short description

Slender species with middorsal spines on segments 4 only, tubes in subdorsal and ventrolateral positions on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, sublateral tubes on segment 8, and very minute laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present in sublateral positions on segment 2. Tergal extensions are relatively long and triangular. Lateral terminal spines acicular; females with short lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plates small.

Key characters and similar species

The combination of middorsal spine on segment 4 only, and segment 2 with tubes in subdorsal and ventrolateral positions is in itself sufficient to distinguish the species from all others. Also the combination of the single middorsal spine, the subdorsal tube and the sublateral glandular cell outlet type 2 on segment 2 leads directly to identification of the species.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described subtidal mud off Vale do Lobo at the south coast of Portugal. The species is only known fromlocalities along the Portuguese Algarve coast.
