Short description

Slender species belonging to the Echinoderes spinifurca

species group. Middorsal spines are present on segments 4 to 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and midlateral tubes on segment 10. All tubes are quite long and well-developed. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present in laterodorsal positions on segments 8 and 9. Lateral terminal spines are acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines and female papillae on sternal plates of segments 7 (ventrolateral) and 8 (ventromedial). Tergal extensions are long, slender and aciculate; trunk length/tergal extension length: 5.1 – 7.4 (average 6.6)%. Sieve plates small.

Key characters and similar species

ies belongs to the Echinoderes spinifurca

species group that all share the same spine/tube pattern (see above) and are characterized by long, acicular tergal extensions, i.e., tergal extensions resembling needles rather than gradually tapering triangular tips. Presence/absence of glandular cell outlets type 2 varies within the group, but if present, they would always be at segments 2, 8 and 9. Species within the group are distinguished by the absence/presence of these outlets, and by differences in trunk length/tergal extension length ratio (=TL/TE ratio).

Among the E. spinifurca species group members, E. sylviaeis so far the only species with glandular cell outlets type 2 in laterodorsal positions on segments 8 and 9, but without such outlets on segment 2. The species can furthermore be recognized by its rather low TL/TE ratio, that is below the ratios of E. kohniand E. yamasakii, and in the lower end of the ranges within E. spinifurca. At the same time its TL/TE proportions are usually higher than in E. microaperturus. Its TL/TE proportions are very close to those in E. lupherorum, but the latter species has glandular cell outlets type 2 in subdorsal positions on segments 2.

Distribution and habitat

from the northern part of the Gulf of Mexico, where it may co-occur with E. spinifurca. Although it has been reported at a single station off Texas, it seems to prefer the cleaner and slightly coarser sediments east of the Mississippi River outlet, towards Alabama and Florida.
