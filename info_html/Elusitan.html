Short description

withlong middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, and lateroventral spineson segments 8 and 9. Tubes on segment 2 in subdorsal andventrolateral positions, on segment 5 in lateroventral positions, on segment 8 in lateral accessory positions, and on segment 10 in laterodorsal positions.

Males with three penile spines, females with minute lateral terminal accessory spines.
Sieve plates slightly larger than the usual round small ones. Tergal extensions roundedand not well defined.

Key characters and similar species

E. lusitanicus has a unique pattern of spines and tubes not shared with any other species, this combined with the reduced lateral accessory spines in females, the rounded short tergal extensions, the absence of glandular cell outlets type 2 and the rectangular trunk shape make it a very easy species to identify.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from the south coast of Portugal from muddy sediments at 100 m depth; posteriorly recorded from the Blanes canyon in the Northeast coast of Spain at 500 to 1500 m depth and from the Gulf of Naples in mud at 100 m depth (unpublished data from Maria Herranz).
