Short description

Species with middorsal spine on segment 4, segment 2 with tubes in subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral, and ventrolateral positions, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 and 7, sublateral tubes on segment 8 (in both sexes, theoriginal description wrongly mentioned that the position of the tubediffers between sexes) and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Lateral terminal spines short and stout, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is easily recognized from a majority of echinoderids bythe presence of middorsal spine only on segment 4 as well as four pairs of tubes on segment 2. In Echinoderes, thecombination of these features is known E. cantabricus, E. ajax, E. capitatus and E. isabelae.
The most conspicuous difference of E. cantabricusto the other three species is the absence of lateroventral spine on segment 8.

Distribution and habitat

The species is recorded from sediment of coarse sand, fine sand/shell gravel or mud at intertidal to shallow waters (at 30 m depth)
around the Iberian Peninsula.
