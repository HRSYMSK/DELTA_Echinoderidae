Short description

middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and small laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in sublateral positions on segment 1, in subdorsal, laterodorsal and sublateral positions on segment 2, and in some specimens but not all, in sublateral positions on segment 8. Tergal extensions are short and triangular, and terminate into short, fringe-like tips. Lateral terminal spines acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plates small.

Key characters and similar species

The presence of glandular cell outlets type 2 in sublateral positions on segment 1 is a rather rare trait, and it easily narrows the number of potential species down to just a few. Besides E. hamiltonorum, only E. anniaeand E. unispinosus have glandular cell outlets type 2 in the lateral series of segment 1.
E. unispinosusis easily distinguished though, by its single middorsal spine and lack of lateroventral spines on segments 8 and 9.
E. anniae also differs from E. hamiltonorumby its lateral accessory glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment 5, and its much longer and pointed tergal extensions.

Distribution and habitat

hamiltonorum is a deep-sea species, known from 2702 to 3 679 m depth off Oregon and California at the US west coast.
