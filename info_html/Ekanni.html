Short description

Slender species with long, pointed tergal extensions with small tooth on inferior margins.
Middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, subdorsal and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, sublateral tubes on segment 8, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are not present. Lateral terminal spines acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plates small.

Key characters and similar species

The combination of five middorsal spines and tubes in subdorsal and ventrolateral positions on segment 2, quickly narrows the field of potential species down to three. Besides E. kanni, only E. adrianoviand E. lusitanicus show this combination. However, E. lusitanicusis easily distinguished from the two because of its plump, nearly parallel-sided trunk, and lack of lateral spines on segments 6 and 7.
E. kanni and E. adrianoviare much more similar, and are only distinguished by more subtle characters. On segment 8 the tubes located in lateral accessory positions in E. adrianovi, whereas they in E. kanniare relocated to more sublateral positions. Furthermore, the middorsal spines in E. kanni gets progressively longer along the trunk segments, whereas the middorsal spine on segment 8 in E. adrianovi actually is shorter than the one on segment 7.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from coral sand from Ghizo Island, Solomon Islands, and is only known from its type locality.
