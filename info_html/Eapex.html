Short description

Species with middorsal spine on segments 4, 6 and 8, segment 2 with tubes in ventrolateral position, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Type 2 glandular cell outlets presentin subdorsal position on segment 2, sublateral position on segment 6 and lateral accessory position on segment 8. Lateral terminal spines long acicular, about 60– 80 % of trunk length. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The spine/tube pattern ofthe species relatively common in Echinoderes, shared with E. bermudensis, E. hamiltonorum, E. joyceae, E. multiporus and E, schwieringae. Among them, all except for E. joyceaeand E. bermudenis differs in theposition of the type-2 glandular cell outlets.
However, E. apex and E. joyceae resemble each other, but differ in longer lateral terminal acicular spines in E. apex (longer than 60% of trunk) thanthose in E. joyceae (shorter than 50% of trunk length). The status of type-2 glandular cell outlets in E. bermudensis is unknown, however, E. apexdistinguished from E. bermudensis alsoin the relative length oflateral terminal spines ( longer than 60% of trunk length in E. apex, whereas shorter than 40% of trunk length in E. bermudensis).

Distribution and habitat

The species is known fromtwo seamounts in NE Atlantic Ocean:the Great Meteor Seamount ( type locality) at 287– 316 m depth, and the Sedlo Seamount at 774– 856 m depth.
