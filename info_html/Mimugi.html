Short description

Meristoderes species with tergosternal junctions in segment 2 appearing as indistinct lines on the cuticular surface, visible with both SEM and LM.
Segment 1 without cuticular hairs except for single hairs associated with the sensory spots.
Middorsal spines are present on segments 4, 6, and 8 (spine on segment 8 nearly reaching beyond terminal segment), subdorsal and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, sublateral tubes on segment 8, lateroventral spines on segments 8 and 9, and minute laterodorsal tubes on segment 10.
Tubes on segment 2 and 8 are very well-developed.
Glandular cell outlets type 2 not present. Tergal extensions are short and triangular. Lateral terminal spines acicular; females with short and thin lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species can be recognized solely by its spine/tube pattern. Especially the combined lack of spines on segments 6 and 7, and the presence of lateroventral spines and sublateral tubes on segment 8 is unique and will immediately lead to species identification. Also the lack of cuticular hairs on segment 1 is quite unusual and a good hint to recognize the species.

Distribution and habitat

is only known from a locality ca. 100 km south of Jeju Island, in the East China Sea.
