Short description

Echinoderes

with rectangular outline of trunk, and acicular spines in middorsal position on segments 4, 6, and 8, and in lateroventral positions on segments 6, 8-9. Tubes present in sublateral positions on segments 2 and 8, lateroventral position on segment 5, and la

terodorsal position on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Pachycycli well-developed along the anterior margins of segments 2-10 and in the margins of sternal plates. Tergal extensions are triangular, thin, and long.

Key characters and similar species

The new species is very easily distinguished from most congeners by its lateral spine series. Unlike the common lateral pattern with lateroventral spines on segments 6-9, E. xtabay lacks lateroventral spines on segment 7, while lateroventral spines are present on segments 6, 8, and 9. This trait is not shared with any other species.

Distribution and habitat

xtabay

is only known from its type locality at 421 m depth in the Bay of Campeche, Gulf of Mexico.
