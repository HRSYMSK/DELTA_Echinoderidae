Short description

Slender species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, laterodorsal tubes on segment 5, and lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present on segment 2 in subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral and ventrolateral positions, on segments 3 to 4 in sublateral positions, on segment 5 in midlateral positions, and on segment 8 in sublateral positions. A middorsal protuberance extends between segments 10 and 11, and tergal extensions triangular and drawn out into thin pointed tips. Lateral terminal spines are long and acicular; females with thin but also rather long lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is quite easily recognized by its relatively large glandular cell outlets in the lateral series on segments 3 to 5.
No other species show this particular combination, and especially with information on spine pattern included as well, the species cannot be confused with any other.

Distribution and habitat

is a deep-sea species with a wide Pacific distribution. It is known from 2702 to 3679 m depth off Oregon and California at the US west coast, but is also abundant at the Hikurangi Margin off New Zealand where it is found on depths from 670 to 1514 m.
It was furthermore reported from 2560 m depth on the continental slope of Chile, in the Southeast Pacific near the Atacama Trench ( Grzelak et al. 2021).
