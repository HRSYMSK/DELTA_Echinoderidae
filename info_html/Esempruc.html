Short description

4, 6 and 8 and lateroventral position onsegments 8 and 9. Tubes in laterodorsal positionon segment 10 (only in males), lateroventral position

can be distinguished from by its unique arrangement ofspines and tubes. In particular the lack of lateroventral spines on segments 6 and 7, is rare and shared only with two congeners:
E. aragorniand E. skipperae. However, these species are easily distinguished from E. semprucciae. E. aragorni

is easily recognised by its numerous minute glandular cell outlets type 2, and E.
skipperae lacks lateral accessory tubes on segment 9 (it has them on segment 8 instead) and has two pairs of ventral tubes on segment 2.

beds ofdecomposing leaves and rhizomesof Neptune grass around the Italian island Ischia at 70 to 8 0 m depth.
