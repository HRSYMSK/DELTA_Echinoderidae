Echinoderes zeppilliae

Echinoderes zeppilliae Sánchezet al.,

Short description

with spines in middorsal position onsegments 4– 8 and lateroventral position on segments 6– 9; tubes inventrolateral positionon segment 2, in lateroventral position on segment 5 and inlaterodorsal position on segment 10; type 2 glandular

cell outlets in subdorsal position on segments 2, 5 and 7 to 9.

Key characters and similar species

recognised by its series of gco2 in subdorsal positions on segment 2, 5 and 7 to 9, combined with five middorsal spinesand long, thin lateral terminal spines (opposite to short, stout ones).

Distribution and habitat

Deep-sea species from areas with manganese nodules at 4092 to 4356 m depth at the Clarion-Clipperton Fracture Zone in the Tropical East Pacific.
