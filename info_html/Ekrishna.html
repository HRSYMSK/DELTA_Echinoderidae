Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segments 2 and 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9 andlaterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present (not mentioned in the original paper) in subdorsal position on segments 2– 8, midlateral position on segments 2 and 9, sublateral position on segment 8, lateral accessory position on segment 6 and lateroventral position on segment 4. Females with ventromedial papillae/pores on segments 7 and 8 ( not mentioned in the original paper), and lateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines long and thin. Sieve plate small. Tergal extensions short, pointed. Sternal extensions large, broadly andlobate, protruding well beyond the tergal extension.

Key characters and similar species

The species iseasily recognized by its broad lobate sternal extensions protruding well beyond the tergal extension.
Also the pattern of the type-2 gland cell outlets is unique in Echinoderes.
In the congeners, the species is similar only to E. cavernus in rounded posterior end on segment 11 as well as the presence of the plenty number of type-2 gland cell outlets.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from its type locality, the sediment at 50 m depth in the English Channel, UK.
