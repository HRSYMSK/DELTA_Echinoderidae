Echinoderes higginsi

Echinoderes higginsi Huys & Coomans, 1989

Short description

Species withmiddorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, laterodorsal and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2 ( presence of the laterodorsal tubes was not mentioned in the original description), lateral accessory tubes on segments 5 (the original description mentioned the tubes on lateroventral position), lateroventral spines on segments 6-9, sublateral tubes on segment 8 (the original description mentioned the tubes onlateral accessory position)
and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent (not mentioned in the original description).
Lateral terminal spines thin long, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species resembles to E. balelrioni, E. hakaiensisand E. newcaledoniensisin having the middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, laterodorsal and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, and lacking any type-2 gland cell outlets. However, E. higginsi differs from thethree species in thelack of the sublateral tubes on segment 2.

Distribution and habitat

The species is recorded fromfine sand at 7.5 m depth in Westerscheldt estuary in the Southern Bight of the North Sea.
