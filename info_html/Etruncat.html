Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, and lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, usual tubes onlaterodorsal position on segment 10. Fringed tubes present various positions and segments:
two pairs in subdorsal and two pairs in laterodorsal, one pair in midlateral, sublateral, andventrolateral position on segment 2, paradorsaland subdorsal position on segment 3, two pairs in subdorsal and one pair in laterodorsal on segment 4, three pairs in subdorsal , one pair in laterodorsal and lateroventral on segment 5, three pairs in subdorsal, one pair in laterodorsal and midlateral position on segment 6, two pairs in subdorsal and one pair in lateral accessory position on segment 7, two pairs in subdorsal, one pair in laterodorsal and lateral accessoryon segment 8, and laterodorsal position on segment 9.
Type-2 gland cell outlets present in laterodorsal position on segments 8 and 9. Lateral terminal spine thin and acicular. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

Echinoderes orestauriwas described as a new species, which was supposed to differ form E. truncatesin having the fringed tubes, the position of the type-2 gland celloutlets. However, after the re-examination of the type material of E. truncatus revealed that E. truncatus hasthe identical characters of E. orestauri. Thus, these two species will be synonymized in afuture study. The speciescan be recognized by having middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, fringed tubes on several positions on segments 2-9, andtype-2 gland cell outlets on segments 8 and 9.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known fromthe sediment at 3 m depth in the Twin Cays, Carrie Bow Cay, Belieze; sand in at 10 m depth in the Bocas del Toro, Caribbean coast of Panama.
