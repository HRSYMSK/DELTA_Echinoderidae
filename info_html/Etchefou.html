Short description

Slender species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateral accessory spines on segment 8, lateroventral spines on segment 9, and very short laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present on segment 2 in subdorsal and lateroventral positions, and on segment 8 inlaterodorsal positions. Lateral terminal spines are acicular, and females have lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is very easily recognized. The combination of middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, but lack of any lateral spines on segments 6 and 7, is only shared with E. eximus. While it is therefore easy to distinguish E.
tchefouensis from other species, it is extremely similar with E. eximus, and it cannot be ruled out that the two species are conspecific.
A possible difference between the two species are the glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment that seem to be in a more dorsal position in E. tchefouensis, i.e. laterodorsal, whereas they are sublateral in E. eximus.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described north east China, but has subsequently proved to have a very wide distribution. It has been collected a numerous localities in the Korea Strait and East China Sea, but also as far away from its type locality as the Northern Marianas, the Philippines, Borneo, and Singapore.
