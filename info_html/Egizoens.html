Short description

Slender species with quite long, gradually tapered tergal extensions, ending in thin seta-like tips. Middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. All tubes are quite strong and well-developed. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are not present.
Sieve plates small.

Lateral terminal spines relatively short and acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines and ventromedial female papillae on sternal plates of segment 7; papillae havecircular openings, and quite broad, sort of rhomboid tubular intracuticular substructures.

Key characters and similar species

The species has an extremely common spine/tube pattern, but still, when combined with the complete absence of glandular cell outlets type 2, it actually resembles only four other species:
E. pilosus, E. spinifurca, E. worthingi, and Echinoderessp. 1 from Yamasaki et al. (2019). However, E. gizoensiscan be distinguished from most of these by its tergal extensions, with flexible, seta-like tips. E. pilosushas no such tips, E. spinifurca has long, aciculate tips, and Echinoderessp. 1 also tends to have rigid, acicular tips. Only E. worthingi seems to have the same tergal extension tips as E. gizoensis, but this

species is relatively easily recognized from all other by its middorsal spines on segment 8 that is considerably longer than all other spines.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from coral sand from Ghizo Island, Solomon Islands, and is only known from its type locality.
