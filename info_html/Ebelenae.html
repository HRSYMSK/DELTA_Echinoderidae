Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, and lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9. Tubes present in subdorsal, midlateral and ventrolateral positions on segment 2, midlateral position on segment 3, laterodorsal position on segment 4, midlateral and lateroventral positions on segment 5, midlateral position on segments 6 – 7, laterodorsal andlateral accessory positions on segment 8, and laterodorsal position on segments 9– 10. Type 2 glandular cell outlets present in sublateral position on segment 2. Lateral terminal spine thick and stout. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species can be easily recognized by the presence of tubes in most of the segments. Especially the presence of tubes on segments 3 and 4 is unique among the all Echinoderes species.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known fromvarious sediment type (coarse sand, shells, sandy mud, mud) in shallow waters (intertidal to 20 m depth) in the Pacific coast of Panama.
