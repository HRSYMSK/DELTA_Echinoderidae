Echinoderes guianensis

Echinoderes guianensis Cepeda et al.,

Short description

with short, poorly sclerotized spinesin middorsal position on segment 4, lateroventral position on segment 6 and sublateral position on segment 7, and tubes in lateroventral position on segment 5 and laterodorsal positionon segment 10. Enlarged, oval sieve plate in sublateral position onsegment 9.

belongs to the Echinoderes coulli-species group.
Within this species group, theabsence of gco2, and combined with presence ofspines in the lateral series of segments 6 and 7 is shared onlywith E. cyaneafictus. The two species can be distinguished by the shape of their sieve plates, which is elongate in E. cyaneafictus, and more oval in E. guianensis. Furthermore, E. cyaneafictushas tubes in lateral accessory positions on segment 8, while E.
guianensishas no tubes on this segment.

Distribution and habitat

the Cayenne and Montsinéry Rivers, French Guiana.
