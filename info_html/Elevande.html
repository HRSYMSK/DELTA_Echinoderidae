Short description

Quite plump species with large, oval sieve plates on segment 9. Middorsal spines are present on segments 4 to 8, with posterior ones being neartwice as long as the anterior ones. Additionally, long and slender tubes are present in subdorsal and ventrolateral positions on segment 2, in sublateral positions on segments 4 and 8, in lateroventral positionson segment 5, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Lateroventral spines are presenton segments 6 to 9. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are not present. Other noteworthy characters include the abovementioned large sieve plates, and segment 10 being completely hairless.
Lateral terminal spines acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines.

Key characters and similar species

The presence of very large sieve plates suggests that this species belongs to the Echinoderes coulli species group, but this is not the case. Another characteristic for this group is the general lack of spines along the trunk, but E. levanderihas well-developed spines. A closer examination of the sieve plates would also reveal that they, despite their size, differ from the E. coulli sieve plate.

Hence, the fastest way to identify E. levanderiis the combination of well-developed spines along the trunk anda pair of very large sieve plates. Other good supplementary characters would be the tubes on segments 2, 4 and 8.

Distribution and habitat

The species lives in the eastern part of the Baltic Sea and in the Gulf of Bothnia. It is a specialized brackish water species, and may tolerate salinities as low as 3 ppt.
