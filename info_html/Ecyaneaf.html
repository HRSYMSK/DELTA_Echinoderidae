Echinoderes cyaneafictus

Echinoderes cyaneafictus Cepeda et al.,

Short description

with short, poorly sclerotized spinesin middorsal position on segment 4 and sublateral positionon segments 6– 7; large tubes in lateroventral position on segment 5,

lateral accessory position on segment 8 and laterodorsal position onsegment 10. Enlarged, oval sieve platesin sublateral position onsegment 9.

belongs to the Echinoderes coulli-species group.
It is distinguished by most other species in this group by its lack of gco2. Among Echinoderes coulli- group species without gco2, Echinoderes cyaneafictus isthe only species with sublateral spines on segments 6 and 7, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10.

Distribution and habitat

mangrove soil in Grande-Terre Island, Mayotte Archipelago, SW Indian Ocean.
