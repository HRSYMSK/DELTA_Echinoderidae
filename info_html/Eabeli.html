Echinoderes abeli

Echinoderes abeli Anguas-Escalante et al., 2023

Short description

with very short and stout lateral terminal spines, middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, and lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9. Tubes present in lateroventral positions on segments 2 and 5, midlateral positions on segment 4, sublateral positions on segment 8, and in laterodorsal positions on segment 10; laterodorsal tubes show sexual dimorphism. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in subdorsal positions segment 2. Posterior margins of segment 11 do not terminate into projecting tergal or sternal extensions. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines, and minute laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Males with long laterodorsal tubes on segment 10 and three pairs of penile spines.

Key characters and similar species

The species differs from a majority of Echinoderidae by its short and stout lateral terminal spines. It can be distinguished from other species with short LTS by the

presence of glandular cell outlets type 2 being restricted to segment 2, as well as the presence of tubes in midlateral positions on segment 4 and sublateral positions on segment 8. These characters are shared with E. augustae only.
These two species are indeed very similar but can be distinguished by their middorsal spine lengths (spines in E. augustae are about 50% longer), differencesin sensory spot distribution, and in the peculiar lack of tergal extensions on segment 11 in E. abeli sp. nov

Distribution and habitat

abeliwas described from Islas de Marias in the Gulf of California. It has so far not been found elsewhere.
