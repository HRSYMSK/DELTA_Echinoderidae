Echinoderes samwisei

Echinoderes samwisei Grzelak and Sørensen, 202 2

Short description

with spines in middorsal position on segments 4, 6 and 8, and spines in lateroventral positions on segments 6 to 9. Tubes present in lateroventral positions on segment 5, lateral accessory positions on segment 8, andlaterodorsal positions on segment 10.
A protuberance-like structure emerges between segments 10 and 11 in middorsal position.

Key characters and similar species

The spine pattern with middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8 and lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9 is very common within Echinoderes, and is shared with 33 species. Nevertheless, out of these only E.
anniae, E.
meteorensis, and an undescribed species, Echinoderessp. 3 from Senghor Seamount (Yamasaki et al. 2019)
resemble E. samwiseisp. nov. by lacking tubes on segment 2.
Thus, none of these species can be confused with E. samwisei.

Echinoderes anniaeand E. meteorensis differ from E. samwisei sp. nov. in having several glandular cell outlets type 2 - structures not observed in E. samwiseisp. nov. Moreover, both species are characterized by very long lateral terminal spines and lack of tubes on segments 5 and 8.
E.
samwisei differs from Echinoderessp. 3 in Yamasaki et al.
(2019)
as well, because the latter lacks tubes on segment 8 and possesses characteristic long and spinose tergal extensions, whereas the new species has lateral accessory tubes on segment 8 and relatively short tergal extensions.

Distribution and habitat

he Hikurangi Margin off New Zealand, where it has been recorded from the continental slopes, as well as deep-sea canyons and seamounts, on depths from 728 to 1350 m depth.
