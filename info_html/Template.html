Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4, 6, 8, segment 2 with tubes in subdorsal (not shown in original description) and ventrolateral positions, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8 and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in sublateral positions onsegment 2 (not shown in original description). Lateral terminal spines short and stout, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is easily recognized from a majority of echinoderids by its short and stout lateral terminal spines. Within the group of Echinoderes with stout lateral terminal spines and middorsal spines on segments 4, 6, and 8, it shows most resemblance with E. belenae, E. brevipes and E. rociae. However, these three latter species all have additional tubes in lateral accessory and in some instances also laterodorsal and midlateral positions, between segments 6 and 8.
E. abbreviatus has only lateral accessory tubes on segment 8.

Distribution and habitat
