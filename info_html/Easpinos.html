Short description

Plump species, probably belonging to the Echinoderes coulli species group. Pectinate fringes on ventral sides of segments 2 to 4 strongly serrated, with obliquely orientated fringe tips pointing towards the midventral articulation. Tubes in sublateral positions on segments 5 and 8 (not mentioned in original description), and otherwise just thin, acicular lateral terminal spines. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plates large and elongate.

Key characters and similar species

The complete lack of spines on segments 1 to 10 makes it easy to distinguish E. aspinosus from all other species. Also the thick, serrated and obliquely orientated pectinate fringe tips in segments 2 to 4 are highly characteristic for this species.

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality at the small Yeonpyeong Island in the Yellow Sea, close to the border between North- and South Korea. The species lives in subtidal sand.
