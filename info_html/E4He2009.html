Short description

Rectangular shaped species with a characteristically short segment 2 and very robust pachycycli. Short middorsal spine on segment 4 andlateroventral spines on segments 5-9. Subdorsal, midlateral, sublateral and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, ventrolateral tubes on segment 5, lateral accessory and subdorsal tubes on segment 8 and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10 in males. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent.
Lateral terminal accessory spines in females absent. Small sieve plate.

Key characters and similar species

The species has a unique pattern of spines and tubes which makes it easy to identify. The species shows most resemblance with E.
isabelae in general appearance and spine/tube pattern, however E. isabelae shows an additional pair of tubes on lateral accessory position on segment 7 absent in E. sp.4.

Distribution and habitat

The species has only been found in Blanes, Spain, in muddy sand at 28-37 m depth.
