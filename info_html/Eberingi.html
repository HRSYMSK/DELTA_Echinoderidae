Echinoderes beringiensis

Echinoderes beringiensis

Adrianov & Maiorova, 2022

Short description

Acicular spines in middorsal position on segments 4– 8, and inlateroventral position on trunk segments 6– 9. Tubes in lateral accessory

position on segment 5. Modified glandular cell outlets type 2 in subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral and ventrolateral positions on trunk segment 2, in midlateral position on trunk segment 5, in midlateral and sublateral positions on

trunk segment 8 in females, and in sublateral position on trunk segment 8 in males.
Trunk segment 11 with two tergal andtwo sternal, cuticular plates.
Trunk segment 11 with middorsal protuberance in males.

Key characters and similar species

Echinoderes beringiensis

shows closest resemblance to the Arctic species E. drogoniand the New Zealand species E.
galadrielae. Thethree species share several features, including tube and spine patterns, number and arrangementof glandular cell outlets type 2 and most sensory spots. They also share another very characteristic and uncommon feature, which is the middorsal division of the tergal plate of segment 11. Nevertheless, despite overall similarity, E. galadrielae differ by its much longer tergal extensions, and E. beringiensiscan be distinguished from E. drogoniby its even shorter tergal extensions, and presence of glandular cell outlets type 2 in laterodorsal positions on segment 10.

beringiensis lives in bacterial mats at 402 to 672 m depths in the Bering Strait, off the Russian autonomous region Chukchi (Chukotka).
