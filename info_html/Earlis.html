Short description

, with characteristic tergal extensions that extend and gradually narrow from triangular bases into very long, rigid tips. Middorsal spines present on segments 4, 6 and 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, sublateral tubes on segment 5 and 8 (presence/absence of tubes on segment 8 show intraspecific variation), lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 (not shown in original description) present in subdorsal and midlateral positions on segment 2.
Lateral terminal spines acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plates small.

Key characters and similar species

The species can be recognized by the combined patterns of spines, tubes and glandular cell outlets type 2, and the conspicuous shape of its tergal extensions. Especially the tergal extensions and the displacement of tubes to sublateral positions on segment 5 and 8, make it easy to distinguish the species.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from the Chukchi Sea, north or Alaska and Kamchatka, and has subsequently been reported from Svalbard.
