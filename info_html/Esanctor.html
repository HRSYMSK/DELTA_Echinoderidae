Echinoderes sanctorum

Echinoderes sanctorum Sánchezet al.,

Short description

with spines in middorsal position onsegments 4– 8 and lateroventral position on segments 6– 9; tubes in ventrolateral positionon segment 2, in lateroventral position on segment 5 and inlaterodorsal position on segment 10; type 2 glandular cell outlets in subdorsal position on segment 1 and in

laterodorsal position on segments 2-9.

Key characters and similar species

The species is easily recognised by its series of gco2 in subdorsal positions on segment 1 and inlaterodorsal position on segments 2-9, combined with five middorsal spines.

Deep-sea species from areas with manganese nodules at 4090 to 4 890 m depth at the Clarion-Clipperton Fracture Zone in the Tropical East Pacific, and the Peru Basin.
