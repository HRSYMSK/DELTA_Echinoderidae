Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, subdorsal and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, laterodorsal tubes on segments 8 and 10. Type 2 glandular cell outlets present in sublateral position on segment 2. Lateral terminal spine short and stout. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species resembles to E. abbreviatus, E. belenae and E. brevipes in the presence of the middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8 and the short and stout lateral terminal spines. However, E. rociae differs from E. abbreviatus in the presence of lateral accessory tubes on segment 7 and laterodorsal tubes on segment 8; from E. belenae in the absence of midlateral tubes on segments 2, 3,5 and 6, and laterodorsal tubes on segments 3 and 9; from E. brevipes in the absence of lateral accessory tubes on segment 6.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from sand in at 10 m depth in the Bastimento Island, Caribbean coast of Panama.
