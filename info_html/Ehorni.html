Short description

Species without middorsal spines, lateroventral tubes on segments 2 and 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6-9, and lateral accessory tubes on segment 8.
Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent.
Lateral terminal spines thin long, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species resembles to E. malakhovi, E. multisetosus, and E. parahorniin the combination of the absence of the middorsal spines and the presence of the lateroventral spines on segments 6-9. Echinoderes horni differs from E. malakhovi in the presence of lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, and from E. multisetosusin the presence of the lateroventral tubes on segment 2 as well as the absence of the lateral accessory tubes on segment 7 and ventromedial tubes on segment 9.

Echinoderes horni and E. parahorni have the identical spine/tube pattern, and sometimes co-occur in the same locality. Thus, they were confused in some previous studies. However, the two species can be distinguished in the type 2 gland cell outlets: absent in E. horni and present in subdorsal position on segment 2 in E. parahorni.

Distribution and habitat

The species is recorded from Caribbean Sea: gray calcareous sediment with detritus and sandor shell gravel at 1-3 m in Belize (type locality); muddy sand at 2-4 m depth in Dominican Republic; and mud at 15 m depth in Puerto Rico.
