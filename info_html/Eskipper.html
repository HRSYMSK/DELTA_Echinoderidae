Short description

Species with trunk showing a nearly rectangular outline, and very thick and conspicuous pachycycli, appearing as transverse belts around the trunk. Middorsal spines are present on segments 4, 6 and 8, sublateral and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5; lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, lateroventral spines on segments 8 and 9, and small, laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 not present. Tergal extensions are curved pinchers, resembling the furcae on an earwig.
Lateral terminal spines are acicular, and females havelateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Distribution and habitat

The species is one of the most abundant in the northern Gulf of Mexico. Its abundance appears to be correlated with the sediment type, and the species seems to prefer the more muddy sediments west of the Mississippi River outlet, rather than the slightly sandier sediments closer to Florida. The species is very abundant from Louisiana to Texas, but has so far never been recorded outsidethe Gulf of Mexico.
