Short description

Slender species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, sublateral tubes on segment 5, sublateral spines on segment 8, lateroventral spines on segment 9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10; specimens may furthermore have sublateral tubes on segment 9, but the presence or absence of these tubes appear to vary within populations – at least among specimens from Svalbard. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present on segment 2 in subdorsal and lateroventral positions, and on segment 8 in midlateral or sublateral positions (not included in original description). Lateral terminal spines are acicu lar, and females have lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is very easily recognized. The combination of middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, but lack of any lateral spines on segments 6 and 7, is only shared with E. tchefouensis.
While it is therefore easy to distinguish E. eximus from other species, it is extremely similar with E. tchefouensis, and it cannot be ruled out that the two species are conspecific. A possible difference between thetwo species are the glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment that seem to be in a more ventral position in E. eximus, i.e. sublateral, whereas they are laterodorsal in E. tchefouensis.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from subtidal mud around Disko Island, West Greenland, but has subsequently been reported from Svalbard where it appears to be the most dominant species.
