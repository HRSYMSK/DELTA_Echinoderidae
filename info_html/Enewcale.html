Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, laterodorsal. sublateral and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segments 6-8, midlateral tubes on segments 8 and 9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 unknown. Females withlateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines long and thin. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species similar to E. brevipesand E. intermediusin the presence of the middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, andlateral accessory tubes on segments 6-8.
However, E. newcaledoniensisis discriminated from them in the presence of sublateral tubes on segment 2, and midlateral tubes on segment 9.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from thesediment of loose coral detritus and fine sandy mud in Baie St. Vincent, New Caledonia (type locality).
