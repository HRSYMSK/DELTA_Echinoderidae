Short description

lump species that has given name the so-called Echinoderes coulli species group, which accommodates (mostly) intertidal species, characterized by shortage of spines and enlarged sieve plates.
No spines are present on segments 1 to 10, and tubes are only present in sublateral positions on segment 5 andlaterodorsal positions on segment 10. Recent re-examinations of the species revealed the presence of numerous minute glandular cell outlets type 2 present on several segments. Lateral terminal spines are always acicular, but Higgins (1977) reports the presence of two different female forms – a Form 1 with moderately short terminal spines, and a Form 2 withvery small, nearly lost, terminal spines. Lateral terminal spines in males are same length as in Form 1 females. Lateral terminal accessory spines are not present in any of the female forms. Sieve plates arelarge and elongate.

Key characters and similar species

The general appearance of the species, and in particular the large, elongate sieve plate easily lead the observer to realize that this species belong to the E. coulli species group. Within this group (but also amongst all other congeners, the species is easily recognized by its completely lack of spines on segments 1 to 10, and only tubes in sublateral positions on segment 5, and in laterodorsal on 10.

Distribution and habitat

was described from tidal channels in saltwater marshes near Georgetown, North Carolina, and it has subsequently been collected marshes in Louisiana.
