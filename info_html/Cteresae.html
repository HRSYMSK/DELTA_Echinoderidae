Short description

Segment 2 of the species with lateroventral articulations and midventral partial articulation. The trunk withmiddorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, segment 2 with tubes insubdorsal positions, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, sublateral spines on segment 7 and lateroventral spines on segments 8 and 9. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Lateral terminal spines thin and long, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

Among Cephalorhyncha, the presence of the middorsal spines only on segment 4, 6 and 8, the sublateral spines on segment 7, as well asthe lateroventral spines only on segments 8 and 9 is known only in C. teresae.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from its type locality Gulf of California, Mexico, from sandy mudat 5 m depth.
