Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, subdorsal tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, andlateral accessory tubes on segment 8. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines long and thin. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species can be identified based on the presence ofmiddorsal acicular spines on segments 4 to 8 and thetubes on segment 2 present only in the subdorsal position. This spine/tube pattern has been known only in E. parrai.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from the northern coast of Spain, Atlantic Ocean (type locality).
