Short description

Meristoderes species with tergosternal junctions in segment 2 appearing as indistinct lines on the cuticular surface (most easily visualized in SEM).
Middorsal spines are present on segments 4, 6, and 8 (spine on segment 8 nearly reaching beyond terminal segment), laterodorsal andventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, and minute laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 not present. Tergal extensions are rather short and triangular.
Lateral terminal spines acicular; females with short and thin lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species can be recognized solely by its spine/tube pattern. Especially the combination of middorsal spines on segments 4, 6, and 8, and the presence of tubes on segment 2 in laterodorsal and ventrolateral positions, is shared only with another yet undescribed species of Meristoderes from Korea. However, this species has a different, much plumper trunk compared to the slender M. herranzae, and then it also has a pair of laterodorsal tubes on segment 8, absent in M.
herranzae.

Distribution and habitat

he species lives in subtidal mud in the Korea Strait and Korea East Sea.
