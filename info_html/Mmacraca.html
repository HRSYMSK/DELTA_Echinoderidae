Short description

rectangular and slender trunk withsegment 2 having paired lateroventral/ventrolateral and midventral partial divisions.
Long middorsal spines onsegments 4, 6 and 8. Ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral on segment 5, lateral accessory onsegment 8 andlaterodorsal on segment 10.
Lateroventral spines on segments 6– 9; long lateral terminal, females withlateral terminal accessory spines.
Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

species is recognized easily by the composition of segment 2 with lateroventral/ventrolateral and midventral partial divisions combined with the spine/tube pattern. Other significant characters are thelack of glandular cell outlets type 2, the very elongated but rectangular trunk shape and the very long middorsal, lateroventral and lateral terminal spines. The middorsal and lateral terminal spines, despite being long and thin, are veryrigid giving the species a particular look.

E. kristenseni highly resembles M. macracanthus sharingthe same spine/tube pattern, however it can be distinguished by the composition of segment 2 as a ring and the presence oftwo pairs of glandular cell outlets type 2 in segment 2 lacking in M. macracanthus.

Distribution and habitat

The species in known from several localities in the Mediterranean Sea including Blanes (NE coast of Spain), La Maddalena Archipelago, Nereo cave and Budelli Island (Sardinia) from coarse sandy to muddy sediments ranging from 4 to 30 m depth. Additionally found in a couple of localities in the Gulf of Naples inmuddy and shell gravel sediment at 24-100 m depth (Unpublished findings by Maria Herranz and Martin V. S ørensen).
