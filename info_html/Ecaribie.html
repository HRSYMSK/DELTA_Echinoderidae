Short description

Species without any middorsal spine, but with and lateroventral tubes/spineson segments 5, 8 and 9. Type 2 glandular cell outlets unknown. Lateral terminal spine thin and long. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate unknown.

Key characters and similar species

Among the all Echinoderes species, E. caribiensishas the unique spine/tube pattern: the presence of spines tubes only lateroventrally on segments 5, 8 and 9, and no middorsal spines.
The absence of middorsal spines and lateroventral spine as well as the presence of lateroventral tubes/spines on segments 5, 8 and 9 suggest the species belongs or is closely related to the Echinoderes coulli species group.
Nevertheless, many characters ( sieve plate, type-2 gland cell outlets) areunknown.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from its type locality, mangrove forest in the Mochima Bay, eastern Venezuela.
