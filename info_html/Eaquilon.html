Short description

Slender species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 5, andlateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9. Glandular cell outlets type 2 (not shown in original description) present in subdorsal, laterodorsal, midlateral and ventrolateral positions on segment 2, subdorsal positions on segment 4, midlateral positions on segment 5, sublateral positions on segment 8, and laterodorsal positions on segment 10. Lateral terminal spines acicularand rather short – 10 to 40% of trunk length. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plates small. A weak indication of a partial midsternal fissure is visible on segment 2.

Key characters and similar species

The species can be recognized by the combined patterns of spines, tubes and glandular cell outlets type 2. It shows very close resemblance to another Arctic species, E. angustus, but can be distinguished by its relatively shorter lateral terminal spines.
It is, however, possible that the two species are synonymous.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from subtidal mud around Disko Island, West Greenland, but has subsequently been reported from Igloolik in Nunavut, Canada, and from Svalbard.
