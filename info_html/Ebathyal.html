Short description

Species with middorsal spineson segments 4 to 8, lateral accessory tubes on segment 5 and lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9. Type 2 glandular cell outlets presentin midlateral position on segment 2 and probably in lateral accessory position on segment 8. Lateral terminal spines very long ( longer than 90% of trunk length), thin andacicular. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species can bedistinguished from thecongeners, except for E. dubiosus, ina combination of spine/tube and type-2 gland cell outlets patternsand very long lateral terminal spines. Echinoderes bathyalisand E. dubiosusare resemblant each other, however, differ in length of lateroventral spines on segment 9 ( spines extending well beyond the terminal segmentin the former, whereas spines extendto the posterior margin of segment 11 in the latter) andslightly displaced tubes on segment 5 (in lateral accessory position in E.
bathyalisand lateroventral position in E. dubiosus).

Distribution and habitat

The species is known fromdeep-sea plain around the Sedlo Seamount, at 2721 – 2875 m depth.
