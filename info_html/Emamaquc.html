Very short, truncate tubesare present in subdorsal and ventrolateral positions on segment 2, lateroventral positions on segment 5, and lateral accessory positionson segment 8; tubes also present inlaterodorsal positions on segment 10, but these are extremely thin, nearly seta-like. Key characters and similar species

Echinoderes mamaquchasp. nov. shows most resemblance with E. kanni and E. adrianovi. Theyshare thesame spine pattern, presence of lateral

accessory tubes on segment 8, dorsal tubes on segment 10 and the absence of glandular cell outlet types 2. Nevertheless, both

species can be distinguished from E. mamaquchaby their markedly longer (up to 2– 4 times) tubes, much shorterand thinner lateral accessory terminal spines, and pointed tergal extensions.
Both species also have veryprominent cuticular perforation sites which are missing in E. mamaqucha. The papillary middorsal flaps present on segments 8 and 9 in E. mamaqucha, are not present in any of the two species.

Distribution and habitat
