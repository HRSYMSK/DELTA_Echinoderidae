Short description

Elongated, cigar-shaped species with 3 very long middorsal spines on segments 4, 6, 8, lateroventral spines on segments 6-9. Subdorsal and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral on segment 5, lateral accessory on segment 8 and laterodorsal on segment 10 in males. Glandular cell outlets type 2 sublateral on segment 2. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species highly resembles E. kristenseni sharing the general appearance and spine pattern, the only difference is the presence of subdorsal tubes on segment 2 in Echinoderes sp.3 whereas in E. kristenseni there is a pair of glandular cell outlets type 2 instead.

Distribution and habitat

The species was only been reported from Blanes, Spain, in muddy sand at 30 m depth.
