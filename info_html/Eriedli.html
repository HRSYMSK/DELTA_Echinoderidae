Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4, 6 and 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Type 2 glandular cell outlets unknown. Lateral terminal spine thin and acicular. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is identical in spine/tube pattern to E. higginsi and E. kristenseni. Although the information on type-2 gland cell outlets in E. riedli and E. higginsi is unknown, E. riedli can be distinguished from these two species in at least in having shorter middorsal and lateroventral spines.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from sandy detritus at near low tide level, the Area of Al-Ghardaqa, Red Sea; and the sediment from the surface of stones covered by Enteromorpha sp. and various diatoms at the Punic Port, Salammbo, Tunisia.
