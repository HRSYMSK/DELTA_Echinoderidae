Short description

Species without middorsal spines, lateroventral tubes on segments 2 and 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6-9, and lateral accessory tubes on segment 8.
Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in subdorsal position on segment 2. Lateral terminal spines thin long, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species resembles to E. horni, E. malakhoviand E. multisetosusin the combination of the absence of the middorsal spines and the presence of the later oventral spines on segments 6-9.
Echinoderes parahorni differs from E. malakhovi in the presence of lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, and from E. multisetosusin the presence of the lateroventral tubes on segment 2 as well as the absence of the lateral accessory tubes on segment 7 and ventromedial tubes on segment 9.

Echinoderes parahorni and E. horni have the identical spine/tube pattern, and sometimes co-occur in the same locality. Thus, E. parahorniwas sometime confused as E. horni. However, the two species can be distinguished in the type 2 gland cell outlets: absent in E. horni and present in subdorsal position on segment 2 in E. parahorni.

Distribution and habitat

The species is recorded fromsandy to silty sedimentat 2-5 m depth in Dominican Republic, the Hispaniola Island (type locality), sand/ shell gravel to mud sediment at 14- 15 m depth off eastern Florida, USA.
