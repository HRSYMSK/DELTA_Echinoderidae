Echinoderes dejesusi

Echinoderes dejesusi

Anguas-Escalante et al., 20 25

Short description

Echinoderes with acicular spines in middorsal positions on segments 4-8, each extending beyond the primary pectinate fringe of their respective segment; middorsal spine on segment 8 also extending beyond the primary pectinate fringe of segment 9. Lateroventral spine

s present on segments 6-9, and tubes present in ventrolateral position on segment 2, lateroventral positions on 5 and in laterodorsal positions on segment 8 and 10. Tubes on segment 8 are thicker. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Tergal extensions short and triangular.

Key characters and similar species

The species shows greatest resemblance to E. kozloffi, E. pacificus, and E. sublicarumdue to its

spine/tube pattern, and long middorsal spines that exceed the posterior margins of their respective segments. However, the thick tubes in laterodorsal positions on segment 8 of Echinoderes dejesusi make it easy to distinguish from the minute glandular cell outlets type 2 in the same positions ofits three congeners.

dejesusiis only known fromits type locality at 421 m depth in the Bay of Campeche, Gulf of Mexico.
