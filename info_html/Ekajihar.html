Short description

medium length middorsal spines on segments 4, 6, and 8; lateroventral tubes on segments 2 and 5; lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 8, lateral spines on segment 9 located lateral accessory (slightly more laterally to lateroventral position), and midlateral tubes on segment 10. Type 2 glandular cell outlets inlaterodorsal position on segments 2 and 5, and subdorsal position on segments 8 and 9. Lateral terminal spines acicular, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

similar to E. multipolusand E. schwieringae in having the samepattern of middorsal spines, lateroventral/ventrolateral tubes and havingthe lateroventral spines on segments 6-8 as well as lateroventral/lateral accessory spines on segment 9. These three species also have ttype-2 gland cell outlets at least on segments 2, 5, 8, and 9. But E. kajiharai can be distingu ished from the remaining two species by its position of the type-2 gland cell outlets.

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality in the Daidokutsu submarine cave, Okinawa, Japan.
