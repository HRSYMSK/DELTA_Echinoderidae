Short description

Very minute (185 µm) and slender species with well-developed tergosternal junctions and partially developed, intracuticular, midsternal line in segment 2. Middorsal spines are present on segments 4 to 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and midlateral spines on segment 1 0. Glandular cell outlets type 2 not present. Pectinate fringes of sternal plates on segments 4 to 9 are very long, covering anterior half of sternal plates on following segment. Tergal extensions are short, triangular and pointed. Lateral terminal spinesare long and acicular; females with thin lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The known morphology of the species is based on a single specimen, mounted for LM. The species is just as small as its dwarfish congeners, C. flosculosa and C. liticola, but C. nybakkeni does not seem to be quite as laterally compressed as these two. However, it does seem to share the same sandy, intertidal beach habitat. The easiest way to distinguish C. nybakkeni from these two, otherwise very similar species, is by its lack of lateral accessory tubes on segment 8.

Distribution and habitat

The species is described from an intertidal locality, aca. 70 deep hole, in a sandy beach at Carmel Beach, California.
