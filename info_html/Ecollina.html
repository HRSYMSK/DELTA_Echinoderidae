Short description

relatively short middorsal spines on segments 4 to 7, lateroventral tubes on segments 2 and 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and later odorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present in laterodorsal positionson segments 8 and 9. Lateral terminal spines long and acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

is easily recognized by its unique middorsal spine pattern, with middorsals on segments 4 to 7, but not on segment 8.

Distribution and habitat

is only known from its type locality in subtidal mud off the Smithsonian Marine Station at Bocas del Toro, (Caribbean) Panama.
