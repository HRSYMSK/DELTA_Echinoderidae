Short description

with spines in middorsal position on segments 4 and 6, and spines in lateroventral positions on segments 6 to 9. Tubesare present in subdorsal, sublateraland ventrolateral positions on segment 2, lateroventral positions on segment 5, sublateral positions on segment 8, and laterodorsal positions on segments 9. Sexually dimorphic tubes furthermore present in laterodorsal positions on segment 10 in males; females with fringe-like structure in midlateral positions. Minute scales present on segments 2 to 10, but regular cuticular hairsare absent throughout trunk.

can be distinguished from most other species by its complete lack of cuticular hairs. This trait is only shaped with Echinoderes leduci and Meristoderes glaber. It can furthermore be recognised byits combination of spines and tubes. Middorsal spines being restricted to segments 4 and 6 only, is rare and shared only with four other species, i.e., E. astridae, E. bispinosus, E. uozumii, and E. landersi. However, E. astridae, E. bispinosus and E. uozumii, lack dorsal and lateral tubes on segment 2, and have instead two pairs of glandular cell outlet type 2 - a gland type that is absent in E. dalzottoi.
E. landersi, in turn, is characterized by four pairs of tubes on segment 2 and three pairs of tubes on segment 8 - a so far unique tube combination.

Distribution and habitat

known from Pahaua - and Honeycomb Canyon at the Hikurangi Range off New Zealand, on depths from 670 to 1171 m.
