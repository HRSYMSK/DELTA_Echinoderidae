Short description

Species with middorsal spine onlyon segment 4, segment 2 with tubes in ventrolateral position, lateral accessory tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 8, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Type 2 glandular cell outlets not present. Lateral terminal spines acicular, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is easily recognized by its unique spine/tube pattern. Among the Echinoderes, the presence ofmiddorsal spines on segment 4 andpaired tubes/spines on segments 2 and 5-8 is shared only with E. ajaxand E. cantabricus.
However, E. antalyaensiscan be distinguished these twospecies in the absence of any tubes on dorsal side on segment 2.

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality in Antalya, Turkey, and was described from red algae, Laurencia obtusa, on sandy bottom at 3 m depth.
