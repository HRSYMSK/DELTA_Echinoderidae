Echinoderes zacharyi

Echinoderes zacharyi Kennedy et al., 202 2

Short description

without acicular spines, except for lateral terminal ones. Midlateral and lateroventral tubes on segment 5; midlateral and lateral accessory tubes on segment 8. Large oval sublateral sieve plate on segment 9. Males with 3 sublateral penile spines, with the lateral-most spine twice as long as the others. Segments 10 and 11 retractable into segment 9.

Key characters and similar species

Echinoderes coulli species group. Within this group, E. zacharyican be distinguished from most species by its lack of gco2 combined with presence of two pairs of tubes on both segment 5 and 8.
Only Echinoderes striihas the same combination of characters, and since itis described from Panama, the distribution ranges of the two species could potentially overlap.
However, they can be distinguished by the midlateral tubes on segment 3, present in E. strii only, and the dorsalmost tubes on segments 5 and 8 that are laterodorsal in E. strii, opposite to midlateral in E. zacharyi.

Distribution and habitat

lives at relatively shallow depths in the Gulf of Mexico. It was described from 4-15 m depths, off Alabama in the northern part of the gulf, but was also recorded from 16 to 21 m depth off Mexico, in the southernmost part of the gulf.
