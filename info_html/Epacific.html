Key characters and similar species

Distinguishing species within the E. dujardinii species group can be really challenging. Species of the group are generally characterised byglandular cell outlets type 2 in laterodorsal or midlateral positons on segments 8 and/or 9, sternal female papillae on certain segments from 6 to 8, short, well-spaced triangular tergal extensions, and the same spine pattern: middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segments 2 and 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and laterodorsal spines on segment 10. The only variation regards lateral accessory spines on segment 8, that are present in E. dujardinii and E. gerardi only.

E. dujardinii group, E. pacificus shows most resemblance with the long spined species, i.e., species where the middorsal spines of segments 5 to 8 surpasses the posterior margin of their respective segments, and especially the middorsal spine of segment 8 surpasses its segment withmore than 40 % percent of its length. Besides E. pacificus these species include, E. kozloffi and E. sublicarum.

These three species are also characterized by the lack of glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment 9 – acharacter that is otherwise present in all other E. dujardinii group species, except the short spined E. songae.

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality at Academy Bay on the Galapagos Islands.
