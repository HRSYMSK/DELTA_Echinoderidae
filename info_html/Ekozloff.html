Key characters and similar species

loffi shares a common tube, spine, glandular cell type 2 pattern and the presence of female papilla with other Echinoderes species which are part or closely related with the E.
dujardinii species group.
Within the E. dujardinii group, E. kozloffi shows most resemblance with the long spined species , i.e., species where the middorsal spines of segments 5 to 8 surpasses the posterior margin of their respective segments, and especially the middorsal spine of segment 8 surpasses its segment with 40-50% percent of its length. Besides E. kozloffit hese species include, E. pacificusand E. sublicarum. These three species are also characterized by the lack of glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment 9 – a character that is otherwise present in all other E. dujardinii group species, except the short spined E. songae.

E. kozloffiis differentiated from E. pacificusand E. sublicarumby the lengths ofits middorsal spines and the positions ofits female papillae.
E. pacificusand E. sublicarum show considerably longer middorsal and lateroventral spines when compared with E. kozloffi.
Regarding the female papillae, both E. pacificus and E. sublicarum lack the ventrolateral pair of segment 6 present in E. kozloffi.

Distribution and habitat

was initially described from intertidal localities in the Pacific coast of the United States and posteriorly recorded from intertidal localities in Calvert Island and Victoria (British Columbia, Canada). The animals were extracted from mud and from brown algae mix with fine sediment.
