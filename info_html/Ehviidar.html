Short description

Rather small species with middorsal spines on segments 6, and 8, tubes on segment 5 in lateroventral positions, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, laterodorsal tubes on segment 9, and very short laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 not present. Middorsal protuberance present between segments 10 and 11.
Lateral terminal spines long and acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The presence of middorsal spines on segments 6 and 8 only is a quite rare combination and only shared with E. daenerysae and the twodeep-sea species E. ultraabyssalis, and Meristoderes okhotensis. Resemblance with the latter is only superf icial though, and the extremely long tergal extensions of M. okhotensis easily distinguish it from the three Echinoderes species. These three species are much more similar though, but E. hviidarumcan be distinguished from them by its lackof tubes on segment 2.

Distribution and habitat

hviidarum is a deep-sea species, known from 2702 to 3 853 m depth off California at the US west coast.
