Short description

Segment 2 withlateroventral incomplete articulations.
Trunk with middorsal spines on segments 6 and 8, subdorsal, midlateral andventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Additional midlateral tubes on segment 8 present only in males. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Lateral terminal spines thin and long, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small. Tergal extensions narrowing abruptly into long acicular tips.

Key characters and similar species

Among the all Echinoderidae species, only M.
okhotensis, E. daenerysae, E.
hviidarum and E.
ultraabyssalis have middorsal spines on segments 6 and 8. However, M. okhotensis can be distin guished from the remaining three speciesin the presence of three pairs of tubes on segment 2 and absence of lateral accessory tubes on segment 8 ( instead males of M. okhotensis have midlateral tubes on segment 8).

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from clay siltat 3351 m depthin the Sea of Okhotsk.
