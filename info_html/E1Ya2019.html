Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, segment 2 with tubes in ventrolateral position, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9 and midlateral tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Female pores/papillae present in ventrolateral position on segment 7 and ventromedial position on segment 8. Lateral terminal spines thinlong, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species resembles E. gizoensis, E. pilosus, E. spinifurca, E. worthingiin spine/tube pattern and lackingany type-2 glandular cell outlets. However, the species can be easily distinguished from them except for E. pilosusin the position of female papillae. In addition, Echinoderes sp.1 differs from E. gizoensis and E. spinifurca in the tergal extension shape (short or long triangular in Echinoderes sp.1, whereas tergal extensions gradually extending into thin tips in E. gizoensisand those are narrowing abruptly into long acicular tips in E. spinifurca), and from E. worthingiin the middorsal spines on segments 4-7 being nearly equal in length (the middorsal spine on segment 8 is more than twice as long as other middorsal spines in E. worthingi). Although the information on gland cell outlets and female pores in E. pilosusis unknown, Echinoderes sp.1 differs from E. pilosusat least in the position of type-1 gland cell outlets on segments 5 and 7 (those in middorsal position in Echinoderes sp.1, whereas in paradorsal/subdorsal position in E. pilosus based on the illustration of the original description of E. pilosus).

Distribution and habitat

The species was recorded fromslope of Senghor Seamount, northeastern Atlantic Ocean, at 565 – 1545 m depth.
