Short description

Species with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateral accessory tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6-9. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateral and ventrolateral position on segment 2, subdorsal and midlateral position on segment 4, midlateral position on segments 5 and 8. Lateral terminal spines thin long, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Segment 2 sometimes with midventral partial articulation. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

Echinoderes ferrugineus resembles to E. pennaki in the spine/tube pattern and the presence of the type-2 glandular cell outlets on segments 2, 4, 5 and 8. However, the former can be distinguished from the latter in the presence of two pairs of type-2 gland cell outlets (in subdorsal and midlateral position) on segment 4 (those are only in subdorsal position on segment 4 in E. pennaki). Echinoderes ferrugineus also similar to E. remanei in spine/tube pattern, however they cannot be distinguished each other in the type-2 gland cell outlet because of the lack of information on that character in E. remanei. The differences between the two species are, (1) the tubes on segment 5 are in lateral accessory position in E. ferrugineus, whereas in lateroventral position in E. remanei, (2) the pectinate fringe on segment 1 is similar to those on segment 2 in E. ferrugineus, whereas that on segment 1 is conspicuously more developed than that on segment 2 in E. remanei.

Distribution and habitat

The species is recorded from algae and sediment of sand, mud or silt at 1-50 m depth around the Italian coasts.
