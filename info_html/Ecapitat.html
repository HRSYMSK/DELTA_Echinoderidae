Short description

Species with middorsal spine on segment 4, lateroventral spines on segments 6-9. Some tubes are often displacedits position or disappeared as intra- or inter-populational variation. Following tubes are present in the all specimens observed so far: subdorsal, midlateral and ventroletaral tubes on segment 2, lateral accessory tubes on segment 5, subdorsal and lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Tubes variationally-present are: laterodorsal tubes on segment 2, subdorsal tubes on segment 6, subdorsal and midlateral tubes on segment 7, and midlateral and ventromedial tubes on segment 8. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Lateral terminal spines short and stout, and females without lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is most similar to E. isabelaein having middorsal spine only on segment 4, lateroventral spines on segment 6-9, andrectangular-shaped trunk withstrong and well-developed pachycycli. The two species are differentiated by the presence/absence of the laterodorsal tubes on segment 10 and sensory spot pattern. However, a future study of comprehensive data on E. capitatus  and E. isabelae may or may not suggest the differences between them are intraspecific variation of a same species (See the discussion in Yamasaki & Dal Zotto 2019).

Distribution and habitat

The species is recorded from sediment of sand, mud or silt at 12-130 m depth around the Italian coasts and the Bay of Kiel, Germany.
