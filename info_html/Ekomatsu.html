Short description

Species with plump trunk shape, and without anymiddorsal spines, lateroventral tubes on segments 5 and 8 and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present on subdorsal (two pairs), laterodorsal (two pairs)
and lateroventral position on segment 2, subdorsal and midlateral position on segment 3, subdorsal, laterodorsal and lateroventral position on segment 4, subdorsal position on segment 5, subdorsal and midlateral position on segment 6, subdorsal, laterodorsal and midlateral position on segments 7 and 8.
Lateral terminal spines long and thin, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate large.

Key characters and similar species

The absence of any spine/tube except for thelateroventral tubes on segments 5 and 8 together with the presence of many small type-2 gland cell outlets characterizes the species. Although these characters allow discriminate the species from the most of the remaining Echinoderes, only two species, E. bengalensis and E. filispinosusare indistinguishable based on these characters because the information on thegland cells is unknown for these two species. So far, the differentiating characters of E. komatsuito the two species are: the shape of the tergal extension (short triangular in E. komatsui, whereas long triangular in E. filispinosus, but unknown in E.
bengalensis) and the relative length of thelateral terminal spine (10-30% of trunk length in E. bengalensis, 30-50% oftrunk length in E. komatsuiand 50-60% of trunk length in E. filispinosus).

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from around the Ryukyu Islands, Japan:
mangrove tidal flat in the Oura Bay, Okinawajima Island (type locality); mangrove tidal flat in Kakeromajima Island; and mangrove tidal flat in Tanegashima Island.
