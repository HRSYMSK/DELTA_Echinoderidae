Short description

The species is typically thickest in the front end, and this, combined with the conspicuously short and stout lateral terminal spines, makes it easy to recognize. Middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, midlateral tubes in segment 4, lateroventral tubes on segment 5; lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and sublateral tubes on segment 8. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present on segment 2 in subdorsal positions.
Conspicuous middorsal protuberance emerge between segments 10 and 11. Lateral terminal spines short and stout, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

tinct shape of the species, combined with the stout lateral terminal spines, the midlateral tubes on segment 4, and the sublaterally displaced tubes on segment 8, make it impossible to confuse this species with anyone else. So far, this is the only species in the Gulf of Mexico with short and stout lateral terminal spines.

Distribution and habitat

Its abundance appears to be correlated with the sediment type, and the species seems toprefer the more muddy sediments west of the Mississippi River outlet, rather than the slightly sandier sediments closer to Florida. The species is very abundant from Louisiana to Texas, but has so far never been recorded outside the Gulf of Mexico.
