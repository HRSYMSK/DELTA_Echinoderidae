Short description

Relatively small slender species with middorsal spines on segments 4 and 7, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. The lateral terminal spinesare neither typical acicular or short and stout. Instead, they are relatively thick and swollen in their proximal halves, but then taper abruptly towards very then distal halves. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Size of sieve plate unknown.

Key characters and similar species

The presence of middorsal spines on segments 4 and 7 only is in itself enough to distinguish this species from all others. However, it might be questioned whether this particular part of the description is correct. All other echinoderids with only two middorsal spines always have them on even numbered segments, and one could speculate if the middorsal spines in E. citrinusare actually on segments 4 and 6. This still needs to be clarified since the species has never been collected again since Zelinka described it, and both type material and photo documentation are unavailable.

Another useful character though is the peculiar shape of the lateral terminal spines, which appear to be unique for this species.

Distribution and habitat

species is described from subtidal mud in the Gulf of Naples.
It has, as mentioned above, not been collected again since its description, despite comprehensive kinorhynch sampling in the Gulf in 2013 and again in 2019.
