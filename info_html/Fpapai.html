Short description

Segment 2 consists of one tergal and two sternal plate divided bylateroventral and midventral articulations.
Trunk with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, lateral accessory tubes on segment 8. Glandular cell outlets type 2. Lateral terminal spines thin and long, and females with ventrolaterally pores/papillae on segment 8 and lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species shares the spine/tube pattern and absence of any type-2 gland cell outlets with Echinoderes muricatus, Cephalorhyncha asiatica, C. flosculosa and C. liticola. However, F.
papai differs from these species in the composition of segment 2: one tergal and two sternal plate divided bylateroventral and midventral articulations in F. papai, whereas complete ring without any articulation in E. muricatusand one tergal and one sternal plates divided by lateroventral articulations andand midventral partial articulation inthe three Cephalorhyncha species.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from at 1849 -1957 m depth west off New Zealand.
