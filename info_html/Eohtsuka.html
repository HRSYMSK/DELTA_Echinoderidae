Short description

Species with middorsal spine on segment 4, lateroventral tubes on segments 5 and 8, lateroventral spines on segments 6 and 7, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10.
Fringed tubes present subdorsal, laterodorsal and ventrolateral position on segment 2, lateral lateroventral position on segment 3, subdorsal andlateroventral position on segment 4, midlateral position on segment 5, laterodorsal position on segment 6, sublateral position on segment 7, and middorsal, laterodorsal, and sublateral position on segment 8.
Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines long and thin. Sieve plate large.

Key characters and similar species

The species/tube pattern ( except for the fringed tubes) of the species exactly identical to E. rex (note: the spines in both species are very small).
However, the two species can be easily distinguished by their lateral terminal spines: thin and long lateral terminal spines in E. ohtsukai, whereas very short lateral terminal spines in E. rex.
The reduced number of spines, the presence of lateroventral tubes on segments 5 and 8, together with the presence of large sieve plates clearly assigns the species to the Echinoderes coulli species group.

Distribution and habitat

The species is known from brackish intertidalmud in the Seto Inland Sea (Japan) and Vancouver (Canada).
