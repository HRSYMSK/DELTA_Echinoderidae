Short description

Species with five middorsal spines on segments 4-10, being the last spine twice as long as the previous ones. Lateroventral spines on segments 6-9, lateroventral tubes on segment 2 and laterodorsal on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 absent.
Sieve plates small. Tergal extensions long and triangular.
Females withlateral terminal accessory spines, and ventromedial female papillae

formed by conspicuously strong spatulate tubular substructures on segments 7 and 8.

Key characters and similar species

The species shares the spine tube pattern E. spinifurcaand E. gizoensis, however the tergal extensions are very long and spiniform in E. spinifurcaand very short and extending into hairs in E. gizoensis while triangular shaped in E. worthingi. Another of the most important characters to identify E. worthingiis the length of the middorsal spines which is nearly the same on segments 4-7 and twice as long on segment 8.

Distribution and habitat

The species was described from Clare Island, Ireland, posteriorly recorded from multiple shallow subtidal areas (mud, shell gravel) along the Atlantic Ocean including localities along the English Channel, (England and France) and Northwest and Southwest coasts of Spain in depths ranging from intertidal to 15 m depth.
