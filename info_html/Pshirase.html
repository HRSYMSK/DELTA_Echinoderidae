Short description

Primary pectinate fringeon segment 1 present along ventromedial and paraventral margin, and strongly serrated.
Segment 2 with midventral and lateroventral complete articulations.
Cuticular hairs and perforation sites absent on all segments.
Trunk with middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8 and lateroventral spines on segments 6-9; of which lateroventral spines on segments 8 and 9 being conspicuously thicker than those on segments 6 and 7.
Rather small spines in various positionson tergal plate ofsegments 4-9: usually present midlaterally on segment 5, subdorsally, laterodorsally and lateral accessoryon segment 6, subdorsally, laterodorsally, sublaterally on segment 7, laterodorsally and lateral accessory on segments 8 and 9: butrarely absent some small spines or additionally present on subdorsal spines on segments 4, 5 and 8.
Similar small spines also present ventromedial position on segments 4-7 and ventrolateral position on segments 8-10.
Tubes on midlateral position on segment 4, lateroventral position on segment 5, and laterodorsal position on segment 10.
Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in midlateral position on segments 6 and 8.
Ventromedial setae present on segment 11.
Lateral terminal spines thin and long, and females with papillae/pores in ventromedial position on segment 6 and ventrolateral position on segment 7, and lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

Among all Echinoderidae species, the Polacanthodres speciesare unique in having many spines on segment, and strongly serrated primary pectinate fringe only in ventromedial to paraventral margin on segment 1. Among the Polacanthoderes, P. shiraseae can be distinguished from P. martineziin having conspicuously thicker lateroventral acicular spines on segments 8 and 9. In addition, P. shiraseae has sublateral small acicular spines on segment 7, whereas P. martinezi has lateral accessory spines in the same segment.
