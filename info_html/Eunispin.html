The species

The speciescan be easily recognized by its single middorsal spine on segment 4 and lateroventral spines on segments 6 and 7.
Several specimens which are the same or similar species to E. unispinosus have been found from various deep -sea localities in Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.

Distribution and habitat

The species was originally found fromsediment at 2875 m depth on thedeep-sea plain near Sedlo Seamount, NE Atlantic Ocean.
Possible same/similar species were reported from: reported as Echinoderessp. 1 from the Anaxinemes Seamount (675-1254 m depth), Mediterranean Sea (Yamasaki et al. 2018); as Echinoderes cf.
unispinosus from the deep sea (2733 -3678 m depth) in off California, NE Pacific (Sørensen et al. 2018). In addition, they have been found in Arctic Sea, Mediterranean Sea, central west and southeast Atlantic Ocean, and Southern Ocean (Yamasaki et al. 2019).
