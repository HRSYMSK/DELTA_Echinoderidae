Short description

pecies with very long, gradually tapered tergal extensions, and midtergal division of segment 11. Middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, and lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are presenton segment 2 in subdorsal, laterodorsal, sublateraland ventrolateral positions, on segments 5 to 7 in midlateral positions, and onsegment 8 in sublateral positions. Lateral terminal spines relatively short and acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species is relatively easily distinguished, despite its stereotypical spine pattern. But the very long tergal extensions and the middorsal division of the terminal segment is very distinct. The tergal extensions taper gradually, which makes the species differ from the E. spinifurca group species that all have acicular tergal extensions.
Also the spine pattern combined with the distribution of glandular cell outlets type 2 is in itself enough to distinguish the species. The species show some resemblance with the seamount species E. kaempfae, but this species does not have glandular cell outlets type 2.

Distribution and habitat

lives in subtidal mud, and it known from the Korea Strait and East China Sea.
