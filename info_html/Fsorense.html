Short description

a rectangular shape with broad sternal plates and very elongated trunk. Segment 2 composed of two sternal and one tergal plate. Four long middorsal spines on segments 4, 5, 6 and 8 andlong lateroventral spines on segments 6 – 9. Broad tubes present in ventrolateral position on segment 2, lateroventralon segment 5 andlaterodorsal on segment 10.
Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in subdorsal position on segment 2 and laterodorsal positionon segments 8 and 9. Males withtwo pairs of crenulated penile spines, females with a pairof lateral terminal accessory spines instead.
Female papillae in ventrolateral and ventromedial positions onsegments 7 and 8. Tergal extensions elongated with pointed tips.

Key characters and similar species

y to distinguish due to the composition of segment 2, and its unique pattern of spines and tubes. It also has a very characteristic elongated and rectangular trunk with broad sternal platesand stiff long spines.

Distribution and habitat

two close by localities 3 milesoff Fort Pierce coast, Florida, USA at 1 0-11 m depth frommuddy sand.
