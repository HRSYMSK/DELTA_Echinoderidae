Short description

Species without any middorsal spines, withventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segments 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, ventromedial tubes on segment 9 and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 unknown. Females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Lateral terminal spines long and thin. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species similar to E.
horni, E. multisetosusand E.
parahorniin the absence of any middorsal spines and the presence of lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9. However, E. malakhovican be distinguished from these three species in the absence of the lateral accessory tubes on segment 8. In addition, E. malakhovi differs from E. multisetosusin the presence of the ventrolateral tubes on segment 2 and the absence of lateral accessory tubes on segment 7.

Distribution and habitat

sand sediment at 60 m depth near the Whale Island, Plenty Bay, North Island, New Zealand.
