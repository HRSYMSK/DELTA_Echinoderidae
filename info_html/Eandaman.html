Echinoderes andamanensis

Echinoderes andamanensis Higgins & Rao, 1979

Short description

Species with short acicular spines middorsally on segment 4 and sublaterally on segment 7 ( not mentioned in the original description).
Tubes present in lateral accessory position on segments 5 ( not mentioned in the original description) and 8, midlateral position on segment 9, andlaterodorsal position on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 present in subdorsal, laterodorsal, and ventrolateral position on segment 2, subdorsal position on segment 4, midlateral position on segments 5 and 7, and laterodorsal position on segments 6 and 8 (not mentioned in the original description). Lateral terminal spines acicular, and females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate large ( not mentioned in the original description).

Key characters and similar species

The species is the most similar to E. annae, sharing almost same spine/tube pattern and identical type-2 gland cell outlets. However, the two species can be distinguished each other in the presence/absence of small acicular sublateral spines on segment 7 (present in E. andamanensis, whereas absent in E. annae), and shape of primary pectinate fringe on ventral side ofsegment 1 (with much shorter tips thanthose on segment 2 in E. andamanensis, whereas tips on segments 1 and 2 are similar length in E. annae).

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from its type locality at Havelock Island, South Andaman Island, India, from intertidal algae.

Literature
