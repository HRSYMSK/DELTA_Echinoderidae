Echinoderes remanei

Echinoderes remanei (Blake, 1930)

Synonymy

Echinoderes tubilak Higgins & Kristensen, 1988; Echinoderes svetlanae Adrianov, 1999; Echinoderes remanei sensu Grzelak et al. 2023

Short description

The species has until recently been very poorly described. A recent study of Grzelak et al. (2023)
revealed several, so far unnoticed structures, which enableda more detailed diagnosis of the species, but also to synonymize it with two other species, E. tubilakand E. svetlanae.

The species is slender, and has middorsal spines on segments 4 to 8, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present on segment 2 in subdorsal, later odorsal, sublateral and ventrolateral positions, on segment 4 in subdorsal positions, on segment 5 in midlateral positions, and on segment 8 in sublateral positions. Pectinate fringe on ventral of side of segment 1 with conspicuously broad fringe tips. Tergal extensions are relatively long and triangular, extending into pointed tips. Lateral terminal spines acicular and not that not; females with lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plates small.

Key characters and similar species

The spine/tube pattern combined with the distribution of glandular cell outlets type 2 easily narrows the field of potential species down to only three candidates, i.e., E. remanei, E. pennaki, and E. obtuspinosus. The latter is very easily distinguished from the other two since it has very short and stout lateral terminal spines.
E. remaneiand E. pennaki

can difficult to distinguish though. Besides the nearly identical spine/tube/gland outlet patterns, they are also the only two species to share the presence of conspicuously broad fringe tips in the pectinate fringe on the ventral side of segment 1. The only differential characters that currently can be pointed of the length of the lateral terminal spines that tend to a little shorter in E. remanei (but ranges are still overlapping though), and the laterodorsal tubes on segment 10 that are present in E. remaneibut absent in E. pennaki.

Distribution and habitat

The species was originally described from Mount Desert Island in Maine, USA, but with the synonymization with E. tubilak and E. svetlanae, the distributional range of the species is increased considerably to Igloolik in Nunavut, Canada, Disko Island in West Greenland and the White Sea in Russia.
