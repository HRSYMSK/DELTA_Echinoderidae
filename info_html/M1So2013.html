Short description

Slightly plump to slender Meristoderes

species with tergosternal junctions in segment 2 appearing as indistinct lines on the cuticular surface, visible at least with SEM. Segment 1 without cuticular hairs except for single hairs associated with the sensory spots. Middorsal spines are present onsegments 4, 6, and 8 (spine on segment 8 nearly reaching beyond terminal segment), laterodorsal and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, laterodorsal and lateral accessory tubes on segment 8, and minute laterodorsal tubes on segment 10. Tubes on segment 2 and 8 are very well-developed. Glandular cell outlets type 2 not present. Tergal extensions are very short and triangular. Lateral terminal spines acicular; females with short and thin lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species can be recognized solely by its distribution of tubes.
Having tubes on segment 2 in only laterodorsal and ventrolateral positions is in itself rather rare, and only shared with three other species, inclusive the congener M. herranzae

. However, when this pattern is combined with the presence of laterodorsal tubes on segment 8 also, it narrows the options down to only this, yet undescribed species. The species is, for now, left as undescribed because only a single specimen, mounted for SEM, was available for examination.

Distribution and habitat

The species is only known from a locality ca. 100 km south of Jeju Island, in the East China Sea.
