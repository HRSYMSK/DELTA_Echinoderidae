Short description

pecies with middorsal spineson segments 4, 6, 8; subdorsal and ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventralon segment 5 andlateral accessory on segments 6– 8, additional laterodorsal tubes onsegment 8 andlaterodorsal, sexually dimorphic tubes on segment 10.
Lateroventral acicular spines on segments 6– 9.
Sublateral glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment 2. Lateral terminal spines long, female with lateral accessory spines. Small sieve plate.

Key characters and similar species

The species is easily recognized by the spine and tube pattern combined with the presence of long lateral terminal spines, and sublateral glandular cell outlets type 2 on segment 2. It shows very close resemblance with E. newcaledonensisand E. brevipes, however E. newcaledonensis shows a midlateral pair of tubes on segment 2, absence of glandular cell outlets type 2 and anadditional pair of midlateral tubes on segment 9. E.
brevipes shares an identical spine/tube/glandular cell outlet type 2 pattern, but it can easily be distinguished from E. intermedius by its short and stout lateral terminal spines.

Distribution and habitat

has beendescribed froma variety of sediments including coral sand, mud and medium sand in shallow areas (1-12 m) of Pacific and Caribbean coasts off Panama. It has also been found in coral sand from shallow areas, in Curacao (unpublished data from Maria Herranz).
