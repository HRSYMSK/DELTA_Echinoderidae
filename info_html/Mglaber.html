Short description

Slender species, with perforation sites on trunk cuticle, but without cuticular hairs on any segment. Tergosternal junctions in segment 2 appearing as distinct andfully developed cuticular fissures. Very long middorsal spines are present on segments 4, 6, and 8 (spine on segment 8 reaching beyond terminal segment), subdorsal (not transversely aligned) and ventrolateral tubes presenton segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateral accessory spines on segment 7, lateroventral spines on segments 8 and 9, and short laterodorsal tubes present on segment 10. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are present in sublateral positions on segment 8. Lateral terminal spines acicular; females withwell-developed lateral terminal accessory spines. Sieve plate small.

Key characters and similar species

The species has a rather unusual spine/tube pattern, and this alone is sufficient to recognize it from all other species. For instance, the combination of having a long, well-developed sublateral spine of segment 7 and nolateral spines on segment 6 is shared only with C. teresae.
Other distinct traits are the complete lack of cuticular hairs, and the prominent tergosternal fissures on segment 2.

Distribution and habitat

30 m depth in the Korean West Sea, near the small Deogjeok Island.
