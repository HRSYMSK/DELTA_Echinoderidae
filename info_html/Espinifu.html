Short description

Slender species with long, acicular tergal extensions that has given name the so-called Echinoderes spinifurca

species group. Middorsal spines are present on segments 4 to 8, ventrolateral tubes on segment 2, lateroventral tubes on segment 5, lateroventral spines on segments 6 to 9, and midlateral tubes on segment 10. All tubes are quite long and well-developed. Glandular cell outlets type 2 are absent. Lateral terminal spines are acicular; females with lateral terminal accessory spines and female papillae on sternal plates of segments 6 (ventromedial) and 7 (ventrolateral).
Tergal extensions are long, slender and aciculate; trunk length/tergal extension length: 4.5 – 15.6 (average 7.8)%. Sieve plates small.

Key characters and similar species

The species belongs to the Echinoderes spinifurca species group that all share the same spine/tube pattern (see above) and are characterized by long, acicular tergal extensions, i.e., tergal extensions resembling needles rather than gradually tapering triangular tips. Presence/absence of glandular cell outlets type 2 varies within the group, but if present, they would always beat segments 2, 8 and 9. Species within the group are distinguished by the absence/presence of these outlets, and by differences in trunk length/tergal extension length ratio (=TL/TE ratio).

Among the E. spinifurca species group members, E. spinifurca is so far the only species without glandular cell outlets type 2. The remaining species have glandular cell outlets type 2 in subdorsal positionson segments 2, and/or in laterodorsal positions on segments 8 and 9. The range of the TL/TE ratio in E. spinifurca

is very broad, especially among the Gulf of Mexico populations, which makes it is less useless for identification compared to other species within the group. The ratio is usually higher than those of E. microaperturusand E. sylviae though, and usually lower than the ratio for E. yamasakii. Within the species group, the distribution of E. spinifurca only overlaps with the one for E. sylviae (both species occur in the Gulf of Mexico), but besides differences in TL/TE ratios, E. sylviaecan be distinguished from E. spinifurcaby its laterodorsal glandular cell outlets type 2 on segments 8 and 9.
